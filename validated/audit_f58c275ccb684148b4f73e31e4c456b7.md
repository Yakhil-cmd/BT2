### Title
Commission Increase Mid-Epoch Retroactively Applies to Entire Epoch's Accrued Rewards — (`src/staking/staking.cairo`)

### Summary
`set_commission` in the Staking contract updates the commission rate immediately without first distributing accrued rewards for the current epoch. When a staker holds an active `CommissionCommitment` that permits a commission increase, they can raise their commission mid-epoch. Because `update_rewards_from_attestation_contract` reads the commission at attestation time and applies it to the **entire epoch's** rewards, delegators lose the yield they had accrued at the lower commission rate for the pre-change portion of the epoch.

### Finding Description

The vulnerability class is an **accounting bug**: a reward-split parameter is mutated without first settling the accrued rewards under the old parameter.

**Root cause — `set_commission` (`src/staking/staking.cairo`, line 725):** [1](#0-0) 

When an active `CommissionCommitment` exists, the internal `update_commission` helper enforces only that `commission <= max_commission` and `commission != old_commission` — it does **not** require the new commission to be lower than the old one: [2](#0-1) 

This means a staker who previously called `set_commission_commitment` with `max_commission > current_commission` can legally **increase** their commission at any point during the commitment window.

**Effect at reward distribution — `calculate_staker_pools_rewards` (`src/staking/staking.cairo`, line 1964):** [3](#0-2) 

The commission is read from storage at the moment `update_rewards_from_attestation_contract` (V2) or `update_rewards` (V3) is called. It is applied to the **full epoch/block reward amount** — there is no checkpoint that records the commission in effect at epoch start. Rewards that accrued while the old (lower) commission was active are retroactively recalculated at the new (higher) commission.

**`set_commission_commitment` allows `max_commission > current_commission`:** [4](#0-3) 

### Impact Explanation

Delegators lose unclaimed yield equal to `(new_commission − old_commission) × epoch_pool_rewards × (time_before_change / epoch_length)`. The staker captures this difference as extra commission. This is a direct theft of unclaimed yield from delegators, matching the **High** impact tier.

### Likelihood Explanation

**Medium.** The staker must:
1. Hold an active `CommissionCommitment` with `max_commission > current_commission`.
2. Call `set_commission` to raise commission mid-epoch, before attestation.

Both steps are permissionless for any registered staker. The commitment mechanism is a documented, publicly callable feature, so the precondition is realistic.

### Recommendation

Before updating the commission in `set_commission` (when the commission is already initialized), distribute the accrued rewards for the current epoch first — analogous to the original report's recommendation to call `distributeRewards` before updating `lastReward`. Specifically, trigger `update_rewards_from_attestation_contract` / `update_rewards` for the staker (if rewards have not yet been distributed for the current epoch) before writing the new commission value to storage. This ensures the pre-change accrual is settled at the old rate.

### Proof of Concept

1. Staker stakes with commission = 500 (5%) and calls `set_commission_commitment(max_commission: 1000, expiration_epoch: current_epoch + 1)`.
2. A new epoch begins; delegators begin accruing rewards at 5% commission.
3. Near the end of the epoch (but before attestation), the staker calls `set_commission(commission: 1000)` — permitted because `1000 <= max_commission` and `1000 != 500`.
4. The attestation contract calls `update_rewards_from_attestation_contract(staker_address)`.
5. Inside `calculate_staker_pools_rewards`, `commission = staker_pool_info.commission()` reads `1000` (10%).
6. The entire epoch's pool rewards are split at 10% commission — delegators receive `pool_rewards * 0.90` instead of `pool_rewards * 0.95`.
7. The staker captures the extra `pool_rewards * 0.05` as commission, which was yield that delegators had accrued at the 5% rate for the full epoch. [5](#0-4)

        fn set_commission(ref self: ContractState, commission: Commission) {
            // Prerequisites and asserts.
            self.general_prerequisites();
            assert!(commission <= COMMISSION_DENOMINATOR, "{}", Error::COMMISSION_OUT_OF_RANGE);
            let staker_address = get_caller_address();
            let staker_info = self.internal_staker_info(:staker_address);
            assert!(staker_info.unstake_time.is_none(), "{}", Error::UNSTAKE_IN_PROGRESS);

            let staker_pool_info = self.staker_pool_info.entry(staker_address);
            if let Option::Some(old_commission) = staker_pool_info.commission.read() {
                self
                    .update_commission(
                        :staker_address, :staker_pool_info, :old_commission, :commission,
                    );
            } else {
                staker_pool_info.commission.write(Option::Some(commission));
                self.emit(Events::CommissionInitialized { staker_address, commission });
            }
        }

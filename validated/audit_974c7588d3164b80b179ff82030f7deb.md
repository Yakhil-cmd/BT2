### Title
`set_commission` Applies Increased Commission Retroactively to the Entire Current Epoch Without Settling Rewards First — (`src/staking/staking.cairo`)

---

### Summary

`set_commission` in `src/staking/staking.cairo` updates the stored commission rate immediately without first settling the current epoch's accrued rewards. Because commission is read and applied at attestation time across the **entire epoch's rewards**, a staker who increases their commission mid-epoch (permitted by the `set_commission_commitment` mechanism) causes the new, higher commission to be applied retroactively to rewards that accrued before the change. Delegators lose yield they legitimately earned under the prior, lower commission rate.

---

### Finding Description

**Step 1 — Commission is stored immediately, no reward settlement occurs.**

`set_commission` reads the caller's staker info, validates the new commission, and writes it directly to storage: [1](#0-0) 

The internal `update_commission` helper performs only validation and then writes the new value: [2](#0-1) 

Neither function calls any reward-accrual or reward-settlement routine before overwriting the commission.

**Step 2 — Commission is read at attestation time and applied to the full epoch's rewards.**

When the staker attests, `calculate_staker_pools_rewards` is invoked. It reads the commission from storage at that moment and uses it to split the **entire epoch's reward pool** between the staker (commission) and delegators: [3](#0-2) 

There is no per-block or per-sub-epoch accounting of which commission rate was in effect at which time. One commission value governs the full epoch.

**Step 3 — The `set_commission_commitment` mechanism permits mid-epoch commission increases.**

Without a commitment, commission can only be decreased. However, once a staker sets a `CommissionCommitment` with a `max_commission` higher than the current rate, `update_commission` allows the commission to be raised to any value up to `max_commission`: [4](#0-3) 

The only constraints during an active commitment are `commission <= max_commission` and `commission != old_commission`. There is no requirement that the commission be lower than the current value.

The code itself acknowledges this gap: [5](#0-4) 

---

### Impact Explanation

A staker who increases commission mid-epoch causes the new, higher commission to be applied to the **entire epoch's rewards** at attestation time, including rewards that accrued during the period when the lower commission was in effect. Delegators receive less STRK than they earned. The staker captures the difference as excess commission.

In the extreme case (commission raised to 10 000 = 100% mid-epoch), delegators receive **zero** rewards for that epoch despite having been delegated for the full epoch at a lower rate. This constitutes **theft of unclaimed yield** from delegators.

Impact classification: **High — Theft of unclaimed yield.**

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The attack requires the staker to have previously called `set_commission_commitment` with a `max_commission` above the current rate. This is a normal, publicly documented operation. Any staker who has opened a pool can execute this sequence without any privileged access, leaked keys, or external dependencies. The staker controls the timing of both the commission increase and the attestation, allowing precise maximization of the retroactive effect.

Likelihood: **Medium** (requires prior commitment setup, but is fully self-contained and permissionless for any staker).

---

### Recommendation

Before writing the new commission value in `set_commission` (or its internal `update_commission` helper), settle the current epoch's rewards for the staker. This ensures the old commission rate is applied to all rewards that accrued before the change, and the new rate only applies going forward.

Concretely, add a call to the reward-update logic (equivalent to what `update_rewards_from_attestation_contract` does) at the top of `set_commission`, before the commission storage write at: [6](#0-5) 

Alternatively, restrict commission changes to take effect only from the **next epoch** by recording the new commission alongside the epoch at which it becomes active, and reading the epoch-appropriate commission in `calculate_staker_pools_rewards`.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Staker stakes with commission `1000` (10%) and opens a pool.
2. Delegator delegates a large amount.
3. Staker calls `set_commission_commitment(max_commission: 10000, expiration_epoch: current_epoch + 1)`.
4. Epoch N begins; rewards start accruing at 10% commission.
5. Mid-epoch N, staker calls `set_commission(10000)` — commission is now 100%.
6. Staker attests within the attestation window.
7. `calculate_staker_pools_rewards` reads `commission = 10000` and applies it to the **entire epoch N reward pool**.
8. Delegators receive `0` pool rewards for epoch N despite having been delegated the entire epoch at 10% commission.
9. The staker captures 100% of the pool-side rewards as commission — the full amount that should have gone to delegators.

The existing flow test `DelegatorUpdatedAfterStakerUpdateCommissionFlow` demonstrates that a commission change mid-epoch does affect the epoch's reward split, confirming the mechanism is live: [7](#0-6)

        fn set_commission(ref self: ContractState, commission: Commission) {
            // Prerequisites and asserts.
            self.general_prerequisites();
            assert!(commission <= COMMISSION_DENOMINATOR, "{}", Error::COMMISSION_OUT_OF_RANGE);
            let staker_address = get_caller_address();
            let staker_info = self.internal_staker_info(:staker_address);
            assert!(staker_info.unstake_time.is_none(), "{}", Error::UNSTAKE_IN_PROGRESS);

            let staker_pool_info = self.staker_pool_info.entry(staker_address);
            if let Option::Some(old_commission) = staker_pool_info.commission.read() {
                self
                    .update_commission(
                        :staker_address, :staker_pool_info, :old_commission, :commission,
                    );
            } else {
                staker_pool_info.commission.write(Option::Some(commission));
                self.emit(Events::CommissionInitialized { staker_address, commission });
            }
        }

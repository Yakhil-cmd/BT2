Audit Report

## Title
Unpermissioned `ClaimOrRefresh` Neuron Age Dilution via Third-Party Stake Injection - (File: rs/nns/governance/src/governance.rs)

## Summary
Any unprivileged principal can call `manage_neuron` with `Command::ClaimOrRefresh { by: By::NeuronIdOrSubaccount }` targeting any existing neuron without authorization. By first transferring ICP to the target neuron's subaccount, the caller triggers `refresh_neuron`, which calls `update_stake_adjust_age` and dilutes the neuron's `aging_since_timestamp_seconds` toward `now` via a weighted-average calculation that assigns zero age to the injected tokens. This permanently reduces the victim neuron's age bonus (up to 1.25× at 4 years for NNS), lowering both its voting power and its share of voting rewards.

## Finding Description
In `manage_neuron_internal` (`rs/nns/governance/src/governance.rs`), the `ClaimOrRefresh` command is dispatched before any ownership check. The `By::NeuronIdOrSubaccount` branch at L6132–6141 calls `refresh_neuron_by_id_or_subaccount` passing only the neuron identifier — the `caller` identity is not forwarded and no authorization gate exists on this path.

`refresh_neuron` (L5900–5962) reads the ledger balance for the neuron's subaccount and, when `balance > cached_neuron_stake_e8s` (i.e., when an attacker has pre-deposited ICP), unconditionally calls `neuron.update_stake_adjust_age(balance.get_e8s(), now)` at L5951. There is no check that the caller is the neuron's controller or a hotkey.

`update_stake_adjust_age` (L999–1040 in `rs/nns/governance/src/neuron/types.rs`) computes the new age via `combine_aged_stakes`, passing `0` as the age of the added tokens. The result is `new_age = old_age × old_stake / (old_stake + added_stake)`. Sending an amount equal to the current stake halves the age in a single operation. Repeated injections drive the age arbitrarily close to zero.

The diluted age feeds directly into `potential_and_deciding_voting_power` (L377–378 in `types.rs`) via `age_bonus_multiplier`, which linearly scales from 1.0× (age = 0) to 1.25× (age ≥ 4 years). The SNS analog (`update_stake` in `rs/sns/governance/src/neuron.rs`, L649–679) applies the same weighted-average age reduction and is reachable via the SNS `ClaimOrRefresh` path with any `controller` principal.

Existing guards are insufficient: the neuron lock at L5911 prevents concurrent refreshes but does not prevent sequential ones; the `min_stake` check at L5925 only ensures the balance meets the minimum, not that the caller owns the neuron.

## Impact Explanation
This matches the High bounty impact category: "Significant NNS/SNS security impact with concrete user or protocol harm." A neuron that has accumulated the maximum 4-year age bonus (1.25×) can have its voting power reduced by up to 20% of total voting power (the age component) in a single attack. Voting rewards (maturity accrual) are proportionally reduced each reward round for as long as the age remains suppressed. For SNS governance, the `max_age_bonus_percentage` is configurable and can exceed 25%, making the impact higher. The victim cannot refuse the stake injection or undo the age dilution. The injected ICP is not stolen — it is added to the victim's stake — but the governance harm (suppressed voting influence and rewards) is real and persistent.

## Likelihood Explanation
The attack entry point is the public `manage_neuron` ingress endpoint, callable by any principal. The neuron subaccount is deterministic and derivable from on-chain history (controller principal + memo). The economic cost scales with the desired impact: halving the age of a neuron with stake S requires sacrificing S e8s permanently. This makes sustained griefing expensive but a targeted one-time dilution (e.g., against a known governance-critical neuron before a significant vote) is feasible. No special privileges, no victim interaction, and no social engineering are required.

## Recommendation
1. **Preserve age on third-party refresh**: In `refresh_neuron`, check whether the caller is the neuron's controller or a registered hotkey. If not, update `cached_neuron_stake_e8s` to reflect the new balance but do not call `update_stake_adjust_age` — leave `aging_since_timestamp_seconds` unchanged.
2. **Gate `By::NeuronIdOrSubaccount` on ownership**: Require the caller to be the controller or a hotkey when using the `NeuronIdOrSubaccount` variant, since this variant does not require knowledge of the memo (the only weak ownership signal for the other variants).
3. **Apply the same fix to SNS**: Propagate the caller identity into `refresh_neuron` in `rs/sns/governance/src/governance.rs` and apply the same ownership check before calling `update_stake` in `rs/sns/governance/src/neuron.rs`.

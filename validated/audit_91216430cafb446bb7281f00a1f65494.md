### Title
Malicious CKD Coordinator Can Produce Corrupted Output Without Cryptographic Enforcement — (File: `src/confidential_key_derivation/protocol.rs`)

### Summary
The CKD protocol designates a single coordinator to collect every participant's masked share and aggregate them into the final `CKDOutput`. No cryptographic mechanism forces the coordinator to aggregate honestly. A malicious coordinator can receive all participants' contributions, inject an arbitrary additive bias into the output, and return a `CKDOutput` that does not correspond to the correct confidential key — causing any downstream consumer to silently derive a wrong key.

### Finding Description

**Protocol structure.** Each participant (including the coordinator) computes a masked share:

```
norm_big_y_i = λ_i · y_i · G
norm_big_c_i = λ_i · (x_i · H(pk, app_id) + y_i · app_pk)
```

Non-coordinator participants send their pair to the coordinator and immediately return `None` — they never learn the aggregated result and have no opportunity to verify it. [1](#0-0)

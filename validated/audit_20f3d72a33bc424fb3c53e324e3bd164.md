### Title
Funds Contributed by `reward_address` via `add_to_delegation_pool` / `increase_stake` Are Permanently Returned to `pool_member` / `staker_address`, Not to the Payer - (`src/pool/pool.cairo`, `src/staking/staking.cairo`)

### Summary

Both `Pool::add_to_delegation_pool` and `Staking::increase_stake` allow the registered `reward_address` to act as the caller and transfer tokens from their own balance into the protocol on behalf of a `pool_member` / `staker_address`. However, when the principal is later returned (via `exit_delegation_pool_action` or `unstake_action`), it is always sent to `pool_member` / `staker_address` — never back to the `reward_address` that actually paid. This is a direct analog of the M-03 misrouting pattern: the payer and the recipient are different addresses, and the protocol unconditionally routes returned funds to the recipient.

---

### Finding Description

**Instance 1 — `Pool::add_to_delegation_pool`**

`add_to_delegation_pool` permits either `pool_member` or `pool_member_info.reward_address` to call it:

```cairo
assert!(
    caller_address == pool_member || caller_address == pool_member_info.reward_address,
    "{}",
    Error::CALLER_CANNOT_ADD_TO_POOL,
);
``` [1](#0-0) 

Funds are then pulled from `caller_address` (which may be `reward_address`):

```cairo
transfer_from_delegator(pool_member: caller_address, :amount, :token_dispatcher);
``` [2](#0-1) 

But `exit_delegation_pool_action` unconditionally returns the principal to `pool_member`, regardless of who funded it:

```cairo
token_dispatcher.checked_transfer(recipient: pool_member, amount: unpool_amount.into());
``` [3](#0-2) 

**Instance 2 — `Staking::increase_stake`**

`increase_stake` similarly allows `reward_address` to be the caller and pulls tokens from them:

```cairo
assert!(
    caller_address == staker_address || caller_address == staker_info.reward_address,
    "{}",
    Error::CALLER_CANNOT_INCREASE_STAKE,
);
...
token_dispatcher.checked_transfer_from(
    sender: caller_address,
    recipient: staking_contract_address,
    amount: amount.into(),
);
``` [4](#0-3) 

But `unstake_action` always returns the principal to `staker_address`:

```cairo
token_dispatcher.checked_transfer(recipient: staker_address, amount: staker_amount.into());
``` [5](#0-4) 

---

### Impact Explanation

When `reward_address` calls `add_to_delegation_pool` or `increase_stake`, it transfers tokens **from its own balance** into the protocol. Those tokens are permanently credited to `pool_member` / `staker_address`. Upon exit, the full principal (including the `reward_address`'s contribution) is returned to `pool_member` / `staker_address`. The `reward_address` has no mechanism to recover the tokens it contributed. This constitutes a **direct, permanent loss of principal** for the `reward_address` — matching the "Direct theft of user funds" Critical impact category.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The `reward_address` role is explicitly granted the ability to call both functions by the access-control checks. A `reward_address` that is a smart contract (e.g., a yield-management contract, a multisig, or a protocol-level compounder) may call `add_to_delegation_pool` or `increase_stake` to top up the stake on behalf of the pool member, reasonably expecting that any principal it contributes will be recoverable. Because the exit path always routes to `pool_member` / `staker_address`, the `reward_address` permanently loses those tokens. The `pool_member` / `staker_address` can then exit and collect the windfall. The likelihood is **medium**: it requires the `reward_address` to contribute its own tokens, but this is an explicitly supported and documented code path.

---

### Recommendation

1. **`exit_delegation_pool_action`**: Track who contributed each increment of `unpool_amount` and return funds to the original contributor, or restrict `add_to_delegation_pool` so that only `pool_member` (not `reward_address`) can be the token source.
2. **`increase_stake`**: Similarly, either restrict the token source to `staker_address` only, or record the contributor and return funds to them on `unstake_action`.
3. The simplest safe fix is to change `transfer_from_delegator` in `add_to_delegation_pool` to always pull from `pool_member` (not `caller_address`), and change `increase_stake` to always pull from `staker_address` (not `caller_address`). This matches the spec's stated pre-condition that "`pool_member` has enough funds." [6](#0-5) [7](#0-6) 

---

### Proof of Concept

**`add_to_delegation_pool` scenario:**

1. `pool_member` registers in the pool with `reward_address = R`.
2. `R` (a smart contract compounder) approves the pool contract for `X` tokens and calls `add_to_delegation_pool(pool_member, X)`.
3. `X` tokens are transferred from `R`'s balance; `pool_member`'s staked balance increases by `X`.
4. `pool_member` calls `exit_delegation_pool_intent(full_amount)` and, after the exit window, calls `exit_delegation_pool_action(pool_member)`.
5. The full `unpool_amount` (including `R`'s `X` tokens) is transferred to `pool_member`.
6. `R` has permanently lost `X` tokens with no recourse.

**`increase_stake` scenario:**

1. `staker_address` registers with `reward_address = R`.
2. `R` approves the staking contract for `X` tokens and calls `increase_stake(staker_address, X)`.
3. `X` tokens are transferred from `R`; `staker_address`'s own stake increases by `X`.
4. `staker_address` calls `unstake_intent()` and, after the exit window, `unstake_action(staker_address)`.
5. The full stake (including `R`'s `X` tokens) is returned to `staker_address`.
6. `R` has permanently lost `X` tokens. [8](#0-7) [9](#0-8)

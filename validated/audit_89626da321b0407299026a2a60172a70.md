### Title
Fixed Gas Limit in `ExitToNear` Precompile `ft_transfer` Promise Causes Permanent Fund Freeze - (File: `engine-precompiles/src/native.rs`)

### Summary
The `ExitToNear` precompile in Aurora Engine attaches a hardcoded `FT_TRANSFER_GAS = 10 TGas` to the NEAR `ft_transfer` promise it creates when bridging ERC-20 tokens out of Aurora. If the target NEP-141 token contract's `ft_transfer` requires more than 10 TGas (e.g., due to hooks, rebasing logic, or complex accounting), the promise fails. Because the ERC-20 tokens are burned on Aurora before the promise is dispatched, and no error-recovery callback is attached in the non-`error_refund` build, the tokens are permanently lost with no on-chain recovery path.

### Finding Description

In `engine-precompiles/src/native.rs`, the `ExitToNear` precompile's `run` function constructs a NEAR promise for the NEP-141 `ft_transfer` (or `ft_transfer_call`) method with a fixed, compile-time constant gas amount: [1](#0-0) 

The gas selection logic is: [2](#0-1) 

`FT_TRANSFER_GAS` is set to exactly 10 TGas. This value was determined experimentally for a simple NEP-141 transfer, but is not sufficient for token contracts with gas-intensive logic.

The callback that could handle a failure and refund the user is only attached when `callback_args != default()`: [3](#0-2) 

The `callback_args.refund` field is only populated when the `error_refund` feature is compiled in: [4](#0-3) 

Without the `error_refund` feature, `refund` is always `None`, and for a plain `ft_transfer` (non-wNEAR), `transfer_near` is also `None`, so `callback_args == default()` and **no callback is attached**. The promise is dispatched as a bare `PromiseArgs::Create` with no failure handler.

The ERC-20 token burn happens inside the EVM execution (the ERC-20 contract calls the precompile as part of its burn function) before the promise is ever dispatched. By the time the NEAR runtime executes the `ft_transfer` promise and it fails due to insufficient gas, the EVM state change (burn) is already committed and irreversible.

### Impact Explanation

**Critical — Permanent freezing of funds.**

A user bridging ERC-20 tokens to NEAR via the `ExitToNear` precompile will have their tokens burned on Aurora with no corresponding credit on NEAR if the NEP-141 `ft_transfer` runs out of the fixed 10 TGas. There is no on-chain recovery mechanism: the NEAR promise failure is silent from Aurora's perspective, and the user cannot retry or reclaim the burned tokens.

Even in the `error_refund`-enabled build, the callback gas is also fixed at `EXIT_TO_NEAR_CALLBACK_GAS = 10 TGas`: [5](#0-4) 

If the refund EVM execution (re-minting the ERC-20) itself requires more than 10 TGas, the refund also fails, resulting in the same permanent loss.

### Likelihood Explanation

**Medium.** The 10 TGas limit is sufficient for standard NEP-141 tokens. However, any NEP-141 token with non-trivial `ft_transfer` logic — such as tokens with transfer hooks, rebasing mechanisms, fee-on-transfer logic, or tokens that trigger downstream cross-contract calls — can exceed this limit. A user who bridges such a token through the `ExitToNear` precompile will permanently lose their funds. The attacker-controlled entry path is simply calling the `ExitToNear` precompile address (`0x...`) from any EVM contract or EOA with a valid ERC-20 burn.

### Recommendation

1. Allow users to specify the NEAR gas to attach to the `ft_transfer` / `ft_transfer_call` promise, analogous to how the XCC precompile allows users to specify gas via `CrossContractCallArgs`.
2. At minimum, increase `FT_TRANSFER_GAS` to a safer value with a documented upper bound, and add a corresponding increase to `EXIT_TO_NEAR_CALLBACK_GAS`.
3. Ensure the `error_refund` feature is always enabled in production builds, and that the callback gas is sufficient for the refund EVM execution.

### Proof of Concept

1. Deploy a NEP-141 token contract whose `ft_transfer` method consumes more than 10 TGas (e.g., by adding a storage write loop or a downstream cross-contract call in the transfer hook).
2. Bridge this NEP-141 token into Aurora via `ft_on_transfer`, receiving ERC-20 tokens.
3. Call the `ExitToNear` precompile from an EVM contract to bridge the ERC-20 tokens back to NEAR. The ERC-20 tokens are burned inside the EVM.
4. The NEAR `ft_transfer` promise is dispatched with only 10 TGas. It fails due to insufficient gas.
5. Without `error_refund`: no callback fires. The ERC-20 tokens are permanently burned on Aurora, and the NEP-141 balance on NEAR is never credited. Funds are permanently frozen.

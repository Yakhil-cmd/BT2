### Title
Missing Zero-Address Validation on `reward_address` in `stake()` and `change_reward_address()` Causes Permanent Freezing of Unclaimed Yield — (`src/staking/staking.cairo`, `src/pool/pool.cairo`)

---

### Summary

`stake()` and `change_reward_address()` in `staking.cairo`, and `enter_delegation_pool()` and `change_reward_address()` in `pool.cairo`, accept a `reward_address` parameter with no check that it is non-zero. A staker or delegator who supplies `address(0)` as their reward address will have all accrued rewards routed to the zero address, permanently destroying them. If the underlying ERC20 reverts on a transfer to `address(0)`, `claim_rewards()` and `unstake_action()` will also revert, temporarily freezing the staker's principal until the reward address is corrected.

---

### Finding Description

`stake()` in `staking.cairo` validates that `reward_address` is not a registered token address, but performs no zero-address check: [1](#0-0) 

`change_reward_address()` in `staking.cairo` applies the same incomplete guard: [2](#0-1) 

`enter_delegation_pool()` in `pool.cairo` similarly only checks that `reward_address` is not the token contract: [3](#0-2) 

When rewards are distributed, `send_rewards_to_staker()` unconditionally transfers to whatever `reward_address` is stored: [4](#0-3) 

`unstake_action()` calls `send_rewards_to_staker()` before returning principal, so a zero `reward_address` causes the entire unstake flow to revert: [5](#0-4) 

Pool `claim_rewards()` has the same direct transfer to the stored `reward_address`: [6](#0-5) 

---

### Impact Explanation

**Permanent freezing of unclaimed yield (High):** If the STRK ERC20 permits transfers to `address(0)` (i.e., does not revert), every reward payment is silently burned. The staker or delegator permanently loses all accrued yield with no recovery path.

**Temporary freezing of funds (High):** If the STRK ERC20 reverts on a transfer to `address(0)`, `claim_rewards()` and `unstake_action()` both revert. A staker who has already called `unstake_intent()` and whose exit window has elapsed cannot execute `unstake_action()` to recover their principal until they first call `change_reward_address()` with a valid address. During any period in which the contract is paused, `change_reward_address()` is also blocked (it calls `general_prerequisites()` → `assert_is_unpaused()`), making the freeze absolute for that window. [7](#0-6) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

Any staker or delegator — unprivileged callers — can trigger this by passing `0` as `reward_address` to `stake()`, `enter_delegation_pool()`, or `change_reward_address()`. No privileged access is required. The entry path is fully reachable. Likelihood is low-to-medium: accidental misuse (e.g., a front-end bug, a scripted deployment with an uninitialized variable) is realistic, and the protocol provides no guard against it.

---

### Recommendation

Add an explicit non-zero assertion on `reward_address` in all four entry points:

- `stake()` in `staking.cairo` [8](#0-7) 
- `change_reward_address()` in `staking.cairo` [2](#0-1) 
- `enter_delegation_pool()` in `pool.cairo` [9](#0-8) 
- `change_reward_address()` in `pool.cairo` [10](#0-9) 

Example fix for `stake()`:
```cairo
assert!(reward_address.is_non_zero(), "Reward address cannot be zero");
```

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Staker calls `stake(reward_address: 0, operational_address: <valid>, amount: min_stake)`.
2. No assertion fires; the staker is registered with `reward_address = 0`. [11](#0-10) 
3. After attestation rewards accrue, staker calls `unstake_intent()`.
4. After the exit window, staker calls `unstake_action()`.
5. `unstake_action()` calls `send_rewards_to_staker()`, which calls `checked_transfer(recipient: 0, amount: rewards)`. [12](#0-11) 
6. **Path A (ERC20 reverts on address(0)):** `unstake_action()` reverts. The staker's principal is frozen. The staker must call `change_reward_address()` to recover, but if the contract is paused at that moment, they cannot.
7. **Path B (ERC20 allows address(0)):** The transfer succeeds, rewards are burned, and the staker permanently loses all unclaimed yield.

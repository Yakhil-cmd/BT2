### Title
Reward Address Change After `exit_delegation_pool_intent` Permanently Blocks `switch_delegation_pool` for Existing Pool Members - (`src/pool/pool.cairo`)

### Summary
A pool member who changes their `reward_address` in either the source or destination pool — after already being a member in both — will be permanently unable to complete `switch_delegation_pool`. The call reverts with `REWARD_ADDRESS_MISMATCH`, leaving the pool member's funds locked in the intent state until the exit window expires.

### Finding Description
`switch_delegation_pool` in `src/pool/pool.cairo` reads the caller's `reward_address` from the **source** pool's `pool_member_info` at call time and bundles it into `SwitchPoolData`: [1](#0-0) 

This data is forwarded through the staking contract to `enter_delegation_pool_from_staking_contract` on the **destination** pool, which enforces: [2](#0-1) 

If the pool member already exists in the destination pool with a **different** `reward_address` (because they called `change_reward_address` there), the assertion fails and the entire transaction reverts.

Critically, `change_reward_address` in the pool contract has **no guard** preventing it from being called while `unpool_time` is set: [3](#0-2) 

This means a pool member can freely change their reward address in either pool at any point — including after calling `exit_delegation_pool_intent` — creating an irreconcilable mismatch that blocks the switch.

### Impact Explanation
The pool member's funds in the source pool are frozen in the intent state. They cannot switch to the desired pool and must wait the full `exit_wait_window` before calling `exit_delegation_pool_action` to recover their principal.

### Title
Malicious Participant Aborts DKG/Reshare/Refresh Without Attribution in `broadcast_success`, Permanently Denying Key Generation — (File: src/dkg.rs)

---

### Summary

In `broadcast_success` (called at the very end of `do_keyshare`), a malicious participant can broadcast `false` or a wrong `session_id` in the final DKG round. The function returns a generic, non-attributing `ProtocolError::AssertionFailed` that does not identify the culprit. Honest parties cannot determine who caused the failure, cannot exclude the malicious participant, and cannot complete key generation, resharing, or refresh.

---

### Finding Description

`broadcast_success` in `src/dkg.rs` is the final step of `do_keyshare`, which backs `keygen`, `reshare`, and `refresh`. It is called **after** every honest participant has already computed their signing share (Step 5.3):

```
// Step 5.3 — signing share already computed
my_signing_share = my_signing_share + signing_share_from.to_scalar();
...
// Step 5.4 and Step 5.5
broadcast_success(&mut chan, &participants, me, session_id).await?;

// Return the key pair
Ok(KeygenOutput { private_share: SigningShare::new(my_signing_share), ... })
``` [1](#0-0) 

`broadcast_success` always broadcasts `(true, session_id)` on behalf of the honest caller:

```rust
let vote_list = do_broadcast(chan, participants, me, (true, session_id)).await?;
```

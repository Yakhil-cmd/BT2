### Title
Missing Maximum Commission Check in `enter_delegation_pool` and `switch_delegation_pool` Allows Staker to Frontrun Delegators — (File: src/pool/pool.cairo)

---

### Summary

Pool members have no way to specify a maximum acceptable commission when entering or switching delegation pools. A staker who has set up a `commission_commitment` with a high `max_commission` can frontrun a delegator's `enter_delegation_pool` or `switch_delegation_pool` transaction by atomically raising their commission to `max_commission` (up to 100%), causing the pool member to earn zero rewards on their delegated stake.

---

### Finding Description

`enter_delegation_pool` accepts only `reward_address` and `amount`: [1](#0-0) 

`switch_delegation_pool` accepts only `to_staker`, `to_pool`, and `amount`: [2](#0-1) 

Neither function accepts a `max_commission` parameter. A pool member who inspects the staker's current commission off-chain and then submits a delegation transaction has no on-chain protection against the commission changing before their transaction is included.

Commission changes take effect **immediately** (no epoch delay): [3](#0-2) 

When a staker has an active `commission_commitment`, `update_commission` permits raising the commission to any value up to `max_commission`: [4](#0-3) 

`set_commission_commitment` allows a staker to set `max_commission` as high as `COMMISSION_DENOMINATOR` (10 000 = 100%): [5](#0-4) 

At reward-calculation time the commission is read live from storage and applied to **all** pool members: [6](#0-5) 

---

### Impact Explanation

If the staker raises commission to 100% (`10000`), `split_rewards_with_commission` routes the entire pool reward to the staker as commission, leaving `pool_rewards = 0`. The pool contract then records a zero reward increment in `cumulative_rewards_trace`, so every pool member earns **zero** rewards for every epoch the commission stays at 100%. This constitutes **theft of unclaimed yield** (High impact): the staker captures all delegation rewards that should have accrued to pool members.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The attack requires the staker to have previously called `set_commission_commitment` with a high `max_commission`. This is a realistic setup: a malicious staker can advertise a low commission (e.g. 5%) to attract delegators, set a commitment with `max_commission = 10000`, and then raise commission to 100% at will during the commitment window. Because Starknet transactions are ordered by the sequencer, the staker can observe a pending `enter_delegation_pool` or `switch_delegation_pool` and submit `set_commission(10000)` ahead of it. No privileged access beyond owning the staker account is required.

---

### Recommendation

Add a `max_commission: Commission` parameter to both `enter_delegation_pool` and `switch_delegation_pool`. At the start of each function, assert that the staker's current commission does not exceed the caller-supplied `max_commission`:

```cairo
let current_commission = self.get_commission_from_staking_contract();
assert!(current_commission <= max_commission, "commission exceeds max");
```

This mirrors the standard slippage-protection pattern (minimum-return check) recommended in the external report.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Staker calls `set_commission_commitment(max_commission: 10000, expiration_epoch: current_epoch + 50)`.
2. Staker calls `set_commission(500)` (5%) to attract delegators.
3. Pool member observes 5% commission and submits `enter_delegation_pool(reward_address, 1_000_000)`.
4. Staker frontruns by calling `set_commission(10000)` (100%); this succeeds because `10000 <= max_commission (10000)` and `10000 != 500`. [4](#0-3) 

5. Pool member's `enter_delegation_pool` executes; funds are transferred and the member is registered. [7](#0-6) 

6. Every subsequent reward update calls `split_rewards_with_commission` with `commission = 10000`, yielding `pool_rewards = 0` for all pool members. [8](#0-7) 

7. Pool member earns zero rewards for the entire duration the commission remains at 100%, while the staker collects all delegation rewards as commission — a direct theft of unclaimed yield.

        ) {
            self.general_prerequisites();
            assert!(max_commission <= COMMISSION_DENOMINATOR, "{}", Error::COMMISSION_OUT_OF_RANGE);
            let staker_address = get_caller_address();
            let staker_info = self.internal_staker_info(:staker_address);
            assert!(staker_info.unstake_time.is_none(), "{}", Error::UNSTAKE_IN_PROGRESS);
            let staker_pool_info = self.staker_pool_info.entry(staker_address);
            assert!(staker_pool_info.has_pool(), "{}", Error::MISSING_POOL_CONTRACT);
            let current_epoch = self.get_current_epoch();
            if let Option::Some(commission_commitment) = staker_pool_info
                .commission_commitment

                assert!(
                    !self.is_commission_commitment_active(:commission_commitment),
                    "{}",
                    Error::COMMISSION_COMMITMENT_EXISTS,
                );
            }
            // Staker must have a commission since it has a pool.
            let current_commission = staker_pool_info.commission();
            assert!(current_commission <= max_commission, "{}", Error::MAX_COMMISSION_TOO_LOW);
            assert!(expiration_epoch > current_epoch, "{}", Error::EXPIRATION_EPOCH_TOO_EARLY);
            assert!(

                "{}",
                Error::EXPIRATION_EPOCH_TOO_FAR,
            );
            let commission_commitment = CommissionCommitment { max_commission, expiration_epoch };
            staker_pool_info.commission_commitment.write(Option::Some(commission_commitment));
            self
                .emit(
                    Events::CommissionCommitmentSet {
                        staker_address, max_commission, expiration_epoch,
                    },
                );
        }

### Title
Commission Change Without Prior Reward Accrual Allows Staker to Steal Delegator Yield - (File: src/staking/staking.cairo)

### Summary

`set_commission()` in `src/staking/staking.cairo` immediately overwrites the stored commission rate without first distributing pending rewards under the old rate. In the pre-consensus (V2) reward regime, rewards for an entire epoch are distributed in a single call to `update_rewards_from_attestation_contract()`. A staker who holds a `CommissionCommitment` can raise their commission to `max_commission` at any point mid-epoch; when the attestation contract later distributes that epoch's rewards, the full epoch's yield is split using the new, higher commission — not the lower rate that was in effect for most of the epoch.

---

### Finding Description

`set_commission()` validates the new commission value and writes it directly to storage: [1](#0-0) 

No reward accrual or distribution step precedes this write.

Reward distribution for the pre-consensus regime happens in `update_rewards_from_attestation_contract()`, which reads the commission at call time via `calculate_staker_pools_rewards()`: [2](#0-1) 

The commission is fetched from storage at the moment the attestation contract triggers distribution — not at the moment the rewards were earned. Because `set_commission()` never calls `update_rewards_from_attestation_contract()` (or any equivalent accrual) before overwriting the rate, the entire epoch's pending yield is retroactively re-split under the new commission.

A `CommissionCommitment` explicitly permits commission *increases* up to `max_commission`: [3](#0-2) 

Without a commitment, commission can only decrease (which would benefit delegators, not harm them). With a commitment, a staker can raise commission at will within the committed ceiling.

The code comment at line 745 acknowledges the gap: [4](#0-3) 

---

### Impact Explanation

**Impact: High — Theft of unclaimed yield.**

A staker with an active `CommissionCommitment` can wait until the final moments of an epoch, raise their commission to `max_commission`, and then allow the attestation contract to call `update_rewards_from_attestation_contract()`. The entire epoch's pool rewards are then split using the elevated commission. Delegators receive far less than they earned under the commission rate that was in effect for the bulk of the epoch. The difference flows to the staker as commission rewards, constituting direct theft of delegator yield.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Likelihood: Medium.**

The attack requires:
1. The staker to have previously called `set_commission_commitment()` with a `max_commission` meaningfully above the current commission.
2. The staker to call `set_commission()` near the end of an epoch to raise commission to `max_commission`.
3. The attestation contract to subsequently call `update_rewards_from_attestation_contract()`.

Steps 1 and 2 are fully staker-controlled. Step 3 is the normal protocol operation and happens every epoch. The staker does not need any special privilege beyond being a registered staker with a pool and a commitment.

---

### Recommendation

Before writing the new commission value in `update_commission()`, distribute any pending rewards for the current epoch using the old commission. Concretely, call the equivalent of `_update_rewards()` (or a dedicated internal accrual helper) with the current commission before overwriting it in storage. This mirrors the fix described in the referenced report (`f43ade3`), where accrual calls were re-added before parameter changes.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Staker calls `set_commission_commitment(max_commission: 5000, expiration_epoch: current + 10)` — commits to allowing up to 50% commission.
2. Staker sets commission to `100` (1%). Delegators delegate, expecting ≤50% commission but observing 1%.
3. Epoch progresses. Delegators earn rewards under 1% commission for the full epoch.
4. One block before the epoch ends, staker calls `set_commission(commission: 5000)`. Storage is updated immediately; no rewards are distributed.
5. Attestation contract calls `update_rewards_from_attestation_contract(staker_address)`. `calculate_staker_pools_rewards()` reads `commission = 5000` and splits the entire epoch's pool rewards 50/50 between staker and pool.
6. Delegators receive ~50% of what they earned; the staker captures the remaining ~49% as commission — despite the 1% rate being in effect for virtually the entire epoch. [5](#0-4) [6](#0-5) [7](#0-6)

        fn update_commission(
            ref self: ContractState,
            staker_address: ContractAddress,
            staker_pool_info: StoragePath<Mutable<InternalStakerPoolInfoV2>>,
            old_commission: Commission,
            commission: Commission,
        ) {
            if let Option::Some(commission_commitment) = staker_pool_info
                .commission_commitment
                .read() {
                if self.is_commission_commitment_active(:commission_commitment) {
                    assert!(
                        commission <= commission_commitment.max_commission,
                        "{}",
                        Error::INVALID_COMMISSION_WITH_COMMITMENT,
                    );
                    assert!(commission != old_commission, "{}", Error::INVALID_SAME_COMMISSION);
                } else {
                    assert!(
                        commission < old_commission, "{}", Error::COMMISSION_COMMITMENT_EXPIRED,
                    );
                }
            } else {
                assert!(commission < old_commission, "{}", Error::INVALID_COMMISSION);
            }

            // Update commission in storage.
            staker_pool_info.commission.write(Option::Some(commission));

            // Emit event.
            self
                .emit(
                    Events::CommissionChanged {
                        staker_address, old_commission, new_commission: commission,
                    },
                );
        }

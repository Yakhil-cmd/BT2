Audit Report

## Title
ckETH Withdrawal Permanently Stuck With No Reimbursement When Gas Fee Exceeds Withdrawal Amount — (`rs/ethereum/cketh/minter/src/withdraw.rs`)

## Summary

The ckETH minter burns a user's ckETH immediately upon `withdraw_eth` acceptance, then defers gas fee validation to transaction-creation time. When the Ethereum gas fee estimate exceeds the withdrawal amount, `create_transactions_batch` reschedules the request indefinitely without ever adding it to the `maybe_reimburse` set. The user's ckETH is permanently destroyed with no ETH sent and no refund issued, violating ledger conservation.

## Finding Description

**Burn-before-fee-check ordering:** `withdraw_eth` in `rs/ethereum/cketh/minter/src/main.rs` burns ckETH immediately and only validates against the static `cketh_minimum_withdrawal_amount`, not the dynamic Ethereum gas fee. [1](#0-0) 

**Fee check deferred to transaction creation:** `create_transaction` in `rs/ethereum/cketh/minter/src/state/transactions/mod.rs` performs `checked_sub(max_transaction_fee)` at transaction-creation time. If the gas fee exceeds the withdrawal amount, it returns `CreateTransactionError::InsufficientTransactionFee`. [2](#0-1) 

**Reschedule-only error handling:** In `create_transactions_batch`, the `InsufficientTransactionFee` arm calls only `reschedule_withdrawal_request` and logs a message. No reimbursement entry is created. [3](#0-2) 

**`maybe_reimburse` never populated on this path:** The only place `maybe_reimburse.insert(withdrawal_id)` is called is inside `record_created_transaction`, which is reached exclusively via the `Ok(transaction)` branch. The `InsufficientTransactionFee` branch never reaches it. [4](#0-3) 

**Faulty assumption exposed by `panic!`:** The `AmountTooLow` ledger burn error handler panics, revealing the developers assumed the only relevant fee floor is the ICRC-1 ledger transfer fee — not the Ethereum gas fee, which is orders of magnitude larger and volatile. [5](#0-4) 

**Recent minimum reduction widens the attack surface:** The minimum withdrawal amount was reduced from 0.03 ETH to 0.005 ETH, narrowing the gap between the minimum and a realistic gas spike (e.g., 238 Gwei × 21,000 gas = ~5,000,000,000,000,000 wei). [6](#0-5) 

The result is an infinite reschedule loop: on every timer tick, `create_transactions_batch` fetches the request, calls `create_transaction`, gets `InsufficientTransactionFee`, and pushes the request back to the queue. No state transition toward reimbursement is ever made.

## Impact Explanation

This is a permanent, irreversible loss of user funds in the ckETH system — a Chain Fusion / ck-token financial integration. ckETH supply decreases (burn is recorded) without a corresponding ETH transfer, breaking the 1:1 backing invariant. Every user who submitted a withdrawal at or near the minimum amount during a gas spike loses their funds with no recovery path. This matches the **High** impact class: "Significant Chain Fusion, ck-token, ledger… security impact with concrete user or protocol harm."

## Likelihood Explanation

No privileges are required. Any user calling `withdraw_eth` with an amount near `cketh_minimum_withdrawal_amount` is at risk if Ethereum gas spikes after the burn. Ethereum gas has historically exceeded 500 Gwei during NFT mints and network congestion events; 238 Gwei is sufficient to trigger the bug at the new 0.005 ETH minimum. The condition is transient but repeatable and requires no attacker — it is triggered by normal network volatility.

## Recommendation

In the `InsufficientTransactionFee` arm of `create_transactions_batch`, after a configurable number of reschedule attempts or a staleness deadline, add the withdrawal ID to `maybe_reimburse` and emit a `ReimbursedEthWithdrawal` event (deducting a small penalty fee), mirroring the reimbursement pattern already used for failed ERC-20 withdrawals. Alternatively, enforce a dynamic minimum at `withdraw_eth` entry by reading the current gas fee estimate and rejecting requests whose amount does not cover `gas_limit × max_fee_per_gas × safety_margin` before burning.

## Proof of Concept

1. Ethereum gas is low; user calls `withdraw_eth` with `amount = 5_000_000_000_000_000` wei (the new minimum).
2. ckETH is burned; `EthWithdrawalRequest` is queued.
3. Ethereum gas spikes to ≥238 Gwei: `max_transaction_fee = 21_000 × 238_000_000_000 = 4_998_000_000_000_000` wei — or any spike above 238 Gwei pushes it over 5_000_000_000_000_000 wei.
4. Timer fires → `create_transactions_batch` → `create_transaction` → `checked_sub` returns `None` → `InsufficientTransactionFee`.
5. `reschedule_withdrawal_request` is called; `maybe_reimburse` is never updated.
6. Steps 4–5 repeat on every timer tick indefinitely.
7. **Observable invariant violation:** total ckETH burned increases; total ETH sent does not; `maybe_reimburse` and `reimbursed` sets contain no entry for this withdrawal ID.

A deterministic unit test can be written using `PocketIC` or the existing `EthTransactions` test harness: create a withdrawal request, call `create_transaction` with a gas fee estimate exceeding the withdrawal amount, assert `InsufficientTransactionFee` is returned, then assert `eth_transactions.maybe_reimburse` does not contain the burn index after `reschedule_withdrawal_request`. [7](#0-6)

* Reduce the minimum ETH withdrawal amount by a factor of 6, from 0.03 ETH (`30_000_000_000_000_000` wei) to 0.005 ETH (`5_000_000_000_000_000` wei) — approximately $10 at current prices. The reasoning is as follows:

At that time ETH traded in a similar USD range (around $2000), but Ethereum mainnet transaction fees were averaging $5–$10 per transaction ([source](https://bitinfocharts.com/comparison/ethereum-transactionfees.html#3y)).
    * Today, Ethereum mainnet fees are in the order of cents and rarely exceed $1.

With current Ethereum fees of ~$0.10–$1, a $10 minimum still preserves the ~10× safety margin even after several fee bumps.

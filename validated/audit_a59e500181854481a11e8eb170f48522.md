### Title
Fee-on-Transfer NEP-141 Token Bridging Mints Excess ERC-20 Tokens, Causing Insolvency - (File: engine/src/engine.rs)

### Summary

`receive_erc20_tokens` and `receive_base_tokens` in `engine/src/engine.rs` unconditionally trust the `amount` field from the `FtOnTransferArgs` callback to determine how many ERC-20 (or base ETH) tokens to mint on Aurora. For a fee-on-transfer NEP-141 token, the NEP-141 contract deducts a fee from the transferred amount so Aurora's actual NEP-141 balance increases by less than `args.amount`, yet Aurora mints the full `args.amount` of ERC-20 tokens. This creates a permanent over-issuance: the ERC-20 total supply exceeds the NEP-141 held in custody, making the bridge insolvent and permanently freezing funds for the last users to exit.

### Finding Description

The NEP-141 bridge entry point is `ft_on_transfer` in `engine/src/contract_methods/connector.rs`. [1](#0-0) 

It reads `args.amount` directly from the JSON input and passes it to either `receive_base_tokens` or `receive_erc20_tokens`. Neither function checks the actual balance change in Aurora's NEP-141 custody account.

In `receive_erc20_tokens`: [2](#0-1) 

`args.amount.as_u128()` is taken verbatim and forwarded to `setup_receive_erc20_tokens_input`, which encodes it as the `amount` argument to the ERC-20 `mint` selector: [3](#0-2) 

This calls `EvmErc20.mint(account, amount)` on the mirror ERC-20 contract: [4](#0-3) 

The same pattern exists in `receive_base_tokens`, which credits `args.amount` as ETH balance without any balance-delta check: [5](#0-4) 

For a fee-on-transfer NEP-141 token, the NEP-141 contract transfers `amount` from the sender but credits Aurora with only `amount - fee`. The NEP-141 standard's `ft_transfer_call` then calls `ft_on_transfer` on Aurora with the original `amount` (the gross figure). Aurora therefore mints `amount` ERC-20 tokens while holding only `amount - fee` NEP-141 tokens in custody. Every such deposit widens the gap between ERC-20 total supply and NEP-141 held, making the bridge progressively insolvent.

When users later call `withdrawToNear` on the ERC-20 (which burns ERC-20 and triggers an `ft_transfer` of NEP-141 back to the user): [6](#0-5) 

the NEP-141 `ft_transfer` will fail for the last users because Aurora's NEP-141 balance is insufficient to cover all outstanding ERC-20 balances. Those users' ERC-20 tokens become permanently unwithdrawable.

### Impact Explanation

**Critical — Insolvency and Permanent Freezing of Funds.**

Every deposit of a fee-on-transfer NEP-141 token mints more ERC-20 tokens than the NEP-141 held in custody. The shortfall accumulates with each deposit. Users who attempt to exit after the NEP-141 reserve is exhausted will have their ERC-20 tokens permanently frozen: the ERC-20 is burned but the corresponding `ft_transfer` on the NEP-141 contract fails, and there is no recovery path in the bridge contract.

### Likelihood Explanation

Any NEP-141 token that implements a transfer fee (a valid and deployed pattern in the NEAR ecosystem) can be registered on Aurora via `deploy_erc20_token` without restriction. The `ft_on_transfer` entry point is callable by any NEP-141 contract that is the `predecessor_account_id` of the cross-contract call, which is exactly the normal bridge flow. No special privilege is required; any token holder can trigger the vulnerability by calling `ft_transfer_call` on a fee-on-transfer NEP-141 with Aurora as the receiver.

### Recommendation

In both `receive_erc20_tokens` and `receive_base_tokens`, replace the use of `args.amount` with the actual balance delta. Before processing the mint, read Aurora's current NEP-141 balance (or ETH balance), and after the transfer completes, compute `actual_received = post_balance - pre_balance`. Use `actual_received` as the mint amount instead of `args.amount`. This is the standard "check received balance" mitigation used in ERC-20 fee-on-transfer contexts.

### Proof of Concept

1. Deploy a NEP-141 token that charges a 1% fee on every transfer (fee deducted from the receiver's credited amount).
2. Call `deploy_erc20_token` on Aurora to register the NEP-141 and create its ERC-20 mirror.
3. Call `ft_transfer_call` on the NEP-141 with `amount = 1000` and `receiver_id = aurora`, `msg = <evm_address>`.
4. The NEP-141 credits Aurora with `990` tokens (1% fee) but calls `ft_on_transfer(sender, amount=1000, msg=...)` on Aurora.
5. Aurora's `receive_erc20_tokens` reads `args.amount = 1000` and mints `1000` ERC-20 tokens to the EVM address.
6. Aurora holds `990` NEP-141 but has issued `1000` ERC-20 tokens — a `10`-token deficit.
7. Repeat step 3–6 many times; the deficit grows proportionally.
8. When the last depositor tries to call `withdrawToNear` for their full ERC-20 balance, the `ft_transfer` on the NEP-141 contract fails because Aurora's NEP-141 balance is insufficient. Their ERC-20 tokens are burned but no NEP-141 is received — funds are permanently frozen.

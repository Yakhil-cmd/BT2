### Title
Missing Zero-Address Validation on `reward_address` in `change_reward_address` and `enter_delegation_pool` — Inconsistent Input Validation Compared to `set_public_key`/`set_peer_id` - (File: `src/staking/staking.cairo`, `src/pool/pool.cairo`)

---

### Summary

The Starknet Staking codebase enforces a non-zero check on `public_key` in `set_public_key` and on `peer_id` in `set_peer_id`, but applies no equivalent non-zero check on `reward_address` in `change_reward_address` (both the staking and pool contracts) or in `enter_delegation_pool`. This inconsistency allows any staker or pool member to register or update their reward address to `0x0`, causing all subsequent reward transfers to revert (or silently burn tokens), permanently or temporarily freezing their unclaimed yield.

---

### Finding Description

`set_public_key` and `set_peer_id` both guard against zero-value inputs: [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1) 

However, `change_reward_address` in the staking contract performs no such check — it only verifies the address is not a registered token: [3](#0-2) 

Similarly, `change_reward_address` in the pool contract only checks the token guard: [4](#0-3) 

And `enter_delegation_pool` in the pool contract also accepts a zero `reward_address`: [5](#0-4) 

The `stake` function in the staking contract likewise accepts a zero `reward_address` and a zero `operational_address`: [6](#0-5) 

When rewards are later claimed, the contract transfers to the stored `reward_address`: [7](#0-6) 

If `reward_address` is `0x0`, the ERC-20 `checked_transfer` call will revert (standard ERC-20 implementations reject zero-address recipients), causing `claim_rewards` to be permanently uncallable until the address is corrected. In the staking contract, `unstake_action` also calls `send_rewards_to_staker` internally, so a zero reward address would block the entire unstake flow.

---

### Impact Explanation

A staker or pool member who (accidentally or deliberately) sets `reward_address` to `0x0` will find that:

1. `claim_rewards` reverts on every call — unclaimed yield is frozen.
2. `unstake_action` reverts because it calls `send_rewards_to_staker` before returning principal — the staker's principal is also temporarily locked.

If the underlying ERC-20 permits transfers to `0x0` (non-standard but possible), rewards are silently burned — **permanent loss of unclaimed yield**.

Impact classification: **High — Permanent or temporary freezing of unclaimed yield / temporary freezing of funds.**

---

### Likelihood Explanation

Any staker or pool member can trigger this by passing `reward_address: 0` to `stake`, `enter_delegation_pool`, or `change_reward_address`. No privileged role is required. The most realistic path is an accidental zero argument (e.g., a misconfigured script or UI bug), but a griefing actor could also self-inflict this to disrupt their own exit flow in ways that affect protocol accounting. Likelihood is **Low-Medium** (user error is plausible; intentional self-griefing is possible).

---

### Recommendation

Add a non-zero guard on `reward_address` in all functions that accept or update it, consistent with the existing guards on `public_key` and `peer_id`:

```cairo
// In change_reward_address (staking), change_reward_address (pool),
// stake, and enter_delegation_pool:
assert!(reward_address.is_non_zero(), "{}", Error::INVALID_REWARD_ADDRESS);
```

Similarly, add a non-zero guard on `operational_address` in `stake`:

```cairo
assert!(operational_address.is_non_zero(), "{}", Error::INVALID_OPERATIONAL_ADDRESS);
```

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Deploy the staking contract and pool contract.
2. Call `stake(reward_address: 0x0, operational_address: <valid>, amount: <min_stake>)` — succeeds with no revert.
3. Advance epochs and attest to accumulate rewards.
4. Call `claim_rewards(staker_address)` — reverts because the ERC-20 transfer to `0x0` fails.
5. Call `unstake_intent()`, wait for exit window, then call `unstake_action(staker_address)` — also reverts for the same reason, locking the staker's principal until `change_reward_address` is called with a valid address.

The same sequence applies to a pool member calling `enter_delegation_pool(reward_address: 0x0, amount: ...)`.

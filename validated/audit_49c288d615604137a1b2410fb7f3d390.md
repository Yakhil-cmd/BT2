### Title
Meta-Transaction Deposit Refund Misdirected to Sender Instead of Relayer on Inner-Receipt Failure — (`runtime/runtime/src/lib.rs`)

---

### Summary

When a meta-transaction (`DelegateAction`) inner receipt fails on the receiver's shard, the deposit refund is sent to the **predecessor of the inner receipt** (the sender/Alice), not to the **relayer** who actually paid the deposit. This creates a direct financial incentive for unprivileged users to craft meta-transactions with large attached deposits designed to fail, draining relayer funds at zero cost to themselves.

---

### Finding Description

NEAR's meta-transaction flow (NEP-366) works as follows:

1. The relayer wraps a `SignedDelegateAction` in a transaction and pays all costs, including any attached deposits in the inner actions.
2. `apply_delegate_action` creates an inner action receipt with `predecessor_id = delegate_action.sender_id` (Alice), not the relayer.
3. This inner receipt is forwarded to the receiver's shard (Bob).
4. If the inner receipt **fails** on Bob's shard, `refund_unspent_gas_and_deposits` generates a deposit refund receipt targeting `receipt.balance_refund_receiver()`.

The critical flaw: `balance_refund_receiver()` returns the **predecessor** of the failing receipt. For meta-transaction inner receipts, that predecessor is **Alice** (the sender), not the relayer who funded the deposit.

The deposit refund path in `refund_unspent_gas_and_deposits`:

```rust
let deposit_refund = if result.result.is_err() { total_deposit } else { Balance::ZERO };
// ...
if deposit_refund > Balance::ZERO {
    result.new_receipts.push(Receipt::new_balance_refund(
        receipt.balance_refund_receiver(),  // ← returns Alice, not the relayer
        deposit_refund,
    ));
}
``` [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1) 

`new_balance_refund` constructs the refund receipt with `receiver_id` set to whoever `balance_refund_receiver()` returns — the predecessor of the inner receipt, which is Alice: [3](#0-2) 

The inner receipt's `predecessor_id` is set to `sender_id` (Alice) inside `apply_delegate_action`, as confirmed by the unit test: [4](#0-3) 

The nearcore documentation explicitly acknowledges this broken invariant:

> "But what if the transaction fails execution on Bob's shard? At this point, the predecessor is `Alice` and therefore she receives the token balance refunded, not the relayer. This is something relayer implementations must be aware of since there is a **financial incentive for Alice to submit meta transactions that have high balances attached but will fail on Bob's shard**." [5](#0-4) 

The root cause is that the deposit-refund routing rule (`refund → predecessor`) is correct for normal receipts but **wrong for meta-transaction inner receipts**, where the predecessor is the user (Alice) rather than the party who funded the deposit (the relayer).

---

### Impact Explanation

**Accounting scope — exact corrupted value:** The deposit amount (an arbitrary `u128` yoctoNEAR value chosen by Alice in her `DelegateAction`) is credited to Alice's account instead of the relayer's account. The relayer's balance is permanently reduced by this amount with no on-chain recourse.

Alice can repeat this attack indefinitely against any relayer, draining its entire NEAR balance. The relayer cannot distinguish a legitimate failing meta-transaction from a malicious one at the protocol level.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

Any unprivileged NEAR account holder can:
- Craft a `DelegateAction` containing a `Transfer` with a large deposit to a non-existent account (guaranteed to fail), or a `FunctionCall` with a deposit to a contract that panics.
- Submit it to any public relayer.
- Collect the deposit refund after the inner receipt fails.

No special privileges are required. The attack is deterministic and repeatable. Off-chain relayer validation cannot fully prevent it due to TOCTOU races (Alice can reduce her FT balance or delete the target account between the relayer's check and the on-chain execution).

---

### Recommendation

The deposit refund for meta-transaction inner receipts should be routed to the **signer of the outer transaction** (the relayer), not to the predecessor of the inner receipt (Alice). This requires one of:

1. Propagating the original relayer's account ID through the receipt chain as a `deposit_refund_receiver` field distinct from `predecessor_id`.
2. Routing deposit refunds for receipts whose `signer_id` differs from `predecessor_id` (the meta-transaction case) to `signer_id` instead of `predecessor_id`.

The gas refund already correctly goes to the relayer (via `signer_id`); the deposit refund should follow the same logic.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Alice creates a `DelegateAction` with `actions = [Transfer { deposit: 1_000_000 NEAR }]` targeting a non-existent account `"void.near"`.
2. Alice signs it and submits it to a public relayer.
3. The relayer wraps it in a transaction, paying 1,000,000 NEAR from its own balance.
4. The inner receipt reaches `"void.near"`'s shard and fails (`AccountDoesNotExist`).
5. `refund_unspent_gas_and_deposits` fires: `deposit_refund = 1_000_000 NEAR`, `balance_refund_receiver() = Alice`.
6. A `new_balance_refund` receipt transfers 1,000,000 NEAR to Alice.
7. The relayer loses 1,000,000 NEAR; Alice gains 1,000,000 NEAR.

The attack maps directly to M-17: just as the Hedger in Symmetrical is incentivized to **not respond** so the user is forced into the `forceClosePosition` path that pays the Hedger a spread, Alice here is incentivized to **submit failing meta-transactions** so the protocol's deposit-refund path pays Alice the relayer's deposit.

### Title
Malicious Coordinator Can Corrupt CKD Output Without Detection by Honest Participants - (File: src/confidential_key_derivation/protocol.rs)

### Summary
In the CKD protocol, all honest participants send their secret shares exclusively to the coordinator and immediately return `Ok(None)`, never receiving or verifying the final aggregated output. A malicious coordinator can silently substitute, omit, or corrupt participant contributions when producing the `CKDOutput`, and no honest participant has any mechanism to detect this. The resulting confidential derived key delivered to the application is unverifiable and may be entirely attacker-controlled.

### Finding Description
The CKD protocol is split into two asymmetric code paths in `src/confidential_key_derivation/protocol.rs`.

**Participant path** (`do_ckd_participant`, lines 17–33):
Each honest participant computes its Lagrange-weighted ElGamal share `(norm_big_y, norm_big_c)` via `compute_signature_share`, sends it **privately and exclusively** to the coordinator via `chan.send_private`, and immediately returns `Ok(None)`.

```rust
// lines 27-32
let (norm_big_y, norm_big_c) =

let waitpoint = chan.next_waitpoint();
chan.send_private(waitpoint, coordinator, &(norm_big_y, norm_big_c))?;
```

The participant never receives any response, never sees the aggregated output, and performs zero verification of the coordinator's work.

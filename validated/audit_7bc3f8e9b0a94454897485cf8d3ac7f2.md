Audit Report

## Title
Cycles Debit Silently Forgiven When Balance Drops Below `ingress_induction_cycles_debit` During DTS Resumed Execution — (`rs/replicated_state/src/canister_state/system_state.rs`)

## Summary
During Deterministic Time Slicing (DTS), ingress induction costs for messages arriving while a canister is paused are recorded as a postponed debit (`ingress_induction_cycles_debit`), validated against `debited_balance()`. However, cycles operations during resumed execution (`ic0.call_cycles_add128`, `ic0.cycles_burn128`) operate against the raw `balance()`, ignoring the pending debit. When `apply_ingress_induction_cycles_debit` is called at execution end, any shortfall is explicitly logged and silently forgiven, violating cycles conservation and allowing a canister to avoid paying the full ingress induction cost.

## Finding Description

**Phase 1 — Debit accumulation uses `debited_balance()` as guard:**

`charge_ingress_induction_cost` checks `debited_balance()` (= `cycles_balance - ingress_induction_cycles_debit`) before appending to the debit: [1](#0-0) 

`add_postponed_charge_to_ingress_induction_cycles_debit` asserts `charge <= debited_balance()`: [2](#0-1) 

At this point the invariant `cycles_balance >= ingress_induction_cycles_debit` holds.

**Phase 2 — Resumed execution uses raw `balance()`, not `debited_balance()`:**

`SandboxSafeSystemState::withdraw_cycles_for_transfer` passes `self.cycles_balance()` — derived from `initial_cycles_balance = system_state.balance()` (raw) — to `CyclesAccountManager::withdraw_cycles_for_transfer`: [3](#0-2) 

`CyclesAccountManager::withdraw_cycles_for_transfer` checks only against the freeze threshold, not against `ingress_induction_cycles_debit`: [4](#0-3) 

`liquid_cycles_balance` (used for `cycles_burn128`) is similarly computed as `cycles_balance() - freeze_threshold`, ignoring the pending debit: [5](#0-4) 

A canister can therefore reduce its balance to `freeze_threshold` (potentially 0) during execution, making `cycles_balance < ingress_induction_cycles_debit`.

**Phase 3 — Debit application silently forgives the excess:**

`apply_ingress_induction_cycles_debit` detects the invariant violation but explicitly continues, forgiving the remaining debit: [6](#0-5) 

The `debug_assert_eq!(remaining_debit.get(), 0)` fires only in debug builds. In production, the error is logged and the excess debit is dropped. `consume_cycles` then saturates the balance to zero, and the forgiven portion is never charged.

## Impact Explanation

This is a concrete cycles conservation violation: the subnet accepts and queues ingress messages whose induction cost is never fully collected. The attacker effectively transfers cycles to a controlled canister without paying the corresponding ingress induction fees. This constitutes illegal minting/theft of cycles at the subnet's expense. The impact maps to **High ($2,000–$10,000)**: a meaningful but constrained attack requiring specific conditions (DTS paused execution), with concrete per-attack cycles gain bounded by the canister's balance. At scale across many paused canisters, the aggregate loss to the subnet is significant.

## Likelihood Explanation

All three required conditions are fully under the control of an unprivileged canister developer: (1) triggering a long DTS update, (2) sending ingress messages while paused to accumulate debit, and (3) calling `ic0.call_cycles_add128` or `ic0.cycles_burn128` to drain the balance below the debit before the slice completes. No special privileges, social engineering, or external compromise is required. The attack is repeatable and automatable. The `debug_assert` and the comment "This case is unreachable" confirm the developers did not intend this path to be reachable, but it is reachable in production.

## Recommendation

`SandboxSafeSystemState::withdraw_cycles_for_transfer` and `liquid_cycles_balance` should account for the pending `ingress_induction_cycles_debit` when computing the spendable balance. Concretely, the effective spendable balance during execution should be `debited_balance()` (i.e., `cycles_balance - ingress_induction_cycles_debit`) rather than the raw `cycles_balance`. The `initial_cycles_balance` passed into `SandboxSafeSystemState` should be `system_state.debited_balance()` instead of `system_state.balance()`. Alternatively, `apply_ingress_induction_cycles_debit` should treat a shortfall as a hard error (panic or abort execution) rather than silently forgiving it.

## Proof of Concept

1. Deploy canister A with freeze threshold = 0 and initial balance = 1,000,000 cycles.
2. Trigger a long-running update on canister A that exceeds one DTS slice.
3. While canister A is paused between slices, send 5 ingress messages to it. Each costs ~100,000 cycles. `charge_ingress_induction_cost` validates each against `debited_balance()` and appends to `ingress_induction_cycles_debit` (total = 500,000). The assert passes because `debited_balance() = 1,000,000 - 500,000 = 500,000 >= 0`.
4. Canister A's Wasm calls `ic0.call_cycles_add128(900,000)` to transfer 900,000 cycles to attacker-controlled canister B. `withdraw_cycles_for_transfer` checks `balance() = 1,000,000 >= freeze_threshold(0) + 900,000` — passes. Balance becomes 100,000.
5. Execution ends. `apply_ingress_induction_cycles_debit` is called: `remaining_debit = 500,000 - 100,000 = 400,000 > 0`. The error is logged, `consume_cycles(500,000)` saturates balance to 0, and 400,000 cycles of debit are forgiven.
6. Net result: attacker moved 900,000 cycles to canister B and paid only 100,000 cycles for ingress induction instead of 500,000 — a gain of 400,000 cycles at the subnet's expense.

A deterministic integration test using PocketIC can reproduce this by: deploying two canisters, triggering a DTS-spanning update on canister A, injecting ingress messages during the pause, and asserting that the final cycle balances violate conservation.

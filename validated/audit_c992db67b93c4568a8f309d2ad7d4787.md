### Title
Unvalidated `d` and `e` Polynomial Shares in Robust ECDSA Presign Allow Malicious Participant to Corrupt Presignature Outputs - (File: src/ecdsa/robust_ecdsa/presign.rs)

---

### Summary

In `do_presign` within the robust ECDSA presign protocol, accumulated `d` and `e` polynomial evaluation shares received from other participants are never validated against any commitment or consistency check. A single malicious participant can send arbitrary scalar values as their `d` and `e` evaluations, corrupting the `alpha` and `e` fields of every honest party's `PresignOutput`. Honest parties accept these corrupted presignatures without any indication of corruption, only discovering the failure when signing is attempted and the coordinator's final signature verification fails.

---

### Finding Description

In `do_presign` (`src/ecdsa/robust_ecdsa/presign.rs`), each participant generates five polynomials — `fk`, `fa`, `fb`, `fd`, `fe` — and sends their evaluations to all other participants in Round 1. [1](#0-0) 

In Round 2, each participant accumulates the received evaluations unconditionally via `shares.add_shares(&package)`: [2](#0-1) 

The `add_shares` method blindly sums all five share components with no validation: [3](#0-2) 

The protocol then performs exponent interpolation checks for the `k` shares (via `big_r_me = g^{k_me}`) and `a` shares (via `big_w_me = R^{a_me}`), and a polynomial interpolation check for `w = a·k + b` against `g^w`. These checks cover `k`, `a`, and `b` shares. However, the `d` and `e` shares are **never validated**. The corrupted values flow directly into the presignature output: [4](#0-3) 

Specifically, `alpha_me = c_me + d_me` (line 319) and `e_me = shares.e()` (line 330) are computed from unverified accumulated `d` and `e` shares. A malicious participant who sends valid `k` and `a` evaluations (to pass all interpolation checks) while sending arbitrary `d` and `e` evaluations will corrupt `alpha_me` and `e_me` for every honest participant, producing an unusable presignature that passes all in-protocol checks.

---

### Impact Explanation

The corrupted presignature is accepted by all honest parties without detection. When signing is subsequently attempted, the coordinator sums the signature shares and calls `sig.verify(&public_key, &msg_hash)`: [5](#0-4) 

This verification fails because `alpha_me` and/or `e_me` are corrupted, causing the signing round to abort. The presignature is permanently wasted. This constitutes **corruption of presign outputs so honest parties accept unusable cryptographic outputs**, matching the allowed High impact: *"Corruption of DKG, reshare, refresh, presign, sign, or CKD outputs so honest parties accept inconsistent public keys, participant sets, transcripts, or unusable cryptographic outputs."*

---

### Likelihood Explanation

Any single malicious participant in the presign protocol can trigger this. The attack requires no special knowledge — only the ability to send arbitrary scalar values as `d` and `e` evaluations while sending valid `k` and `a` evaluations to pass the exponent interpolation checks. The malicious participant is not identified or excluded, and the corruption is undetectable until signing fails. The attack is trivially repeatable across every presign invocation, permanently blocking signing for honest parties as long as the malicious participant remains in the set.

---

### Recommendation

Add commitment-based validation for `d` and `e` shares. Participants should broadcast polynomial commitments (group element vectors) for their `fd` and `fe` polynomials alongside their `fk`/`fa` commitments, and each honest party should verify that received `d_j` and `e_j` evaluations are consistent with the committed polynomials before accumulating them. Alternatively, apply the same exponent interpolation consistency check already used for `R` and `W` to the `d` and `e` share vectors, verifying that the received evaluations lie on a degree-`2t` polynomial with zero constant term.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Malicious participant M generates valid `fk`, `fa`, `fb` polynomials of the required degrees.
2. M generates arbitrary (non-zero-secret) `fd'` and `fe'` polynomials — e.g., with a non-zero constant term.
3. M sends valid evaluations of `fk`, `fa`, `fb` to all honest participants (so `big_r_me` and `big_w_me` exponent interpolation checks pass).
4. M sends corrupted evaluations of `fd'`, `fe'` to all honest participants.
5. Honest participants call `shares.add_shares(&package)` unconditionally, accumulating the corrupted `d` and `e` values.
6. All exponent interpolation checks for `R` and `W` pass (since `k` and `a` shares are valid); the `W == g^w` check also passes (since `b` shares are valid).
7. Each honest participant computes `alpha_me = c_me + d_me` where `d_me` is corrupted, and returns `e_me = shares.e()` which is also corrupted.
8. All honest parties return a `PresignOutput` with corrupted `alpha` and `e` fields — accepted without error.
9. When signing is attempted, `compute_signature_share` produces an incorrect `s_me`, the coordinator's aggregated `s` fails `sig.verify()`, and signing aborts.
10. All presignatures are permanently wasted; the presign protocol must be rerun, and M can repeat the attack indefinitely. [2](#0-1) [4](#0-3)

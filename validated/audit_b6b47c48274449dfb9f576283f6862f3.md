### Title
XCC Router Storage-Staking NEAR Permanently Inaccessible Through EVM Interface — (File: `engine-precompiles/src/xcc.rs`)

---

### Summary

When an EVM user first invokes the XCC precompile, `STORAGE_AMOUNT` (2 NEAR) worth of wNEAR is automatically deducted from their balance to fund the router sub-account's storage staking. The XCC precompile only ever creates promises for the `execute` and `schedule` methods on the router. No EVM-accessible path exists to withdraw that NEAR back, close the router, or otherwise opt out. The 2 NEAR is permanently frozen from the EVM user's perspective.

---

### Finding Description

**Step 1 — Automatic charge on first use.**

On the first call to the XCC precompile, the code detects that no router has been deployed for the caller and adds `STORAGE_AMOUNT` to the required payment: [1](#0-0) 

`STORAGE_AMOUNT` is defined as 2 × 10²⁴ yoctoNEAR (2 NEAR): [2](#0-1) 

The precompile then calls `transferFrom` on the wNEAR ERC-20 contract to pull `required_near` from the caller into the engine's implicit address: [3](#0-2) 

**Step 2 — NEAR is locked in the router sub-account.**

`handle_precompile_promise` in `engine/src/xcc.rs` deploys the router and transfers `STORAGE_AMOUNT` to it as storage-staking collateral: [4](#0-3) 

**Step 3 — Only `execute` and `schedule` are reachable through the precompile.**

The precompile constructs promises exclusively for these two router methods: [5](#0-4) 

No other router method (e.g., a withdrawal or account-close function) is reachable through any EVM call path. `handle_precompile_promise` validates that every promise targets the router sub-account and originates from the XCC precompile, so a user cannot craft a promise to call any other method: [6](#0-5) 

**Step 4 — No opt-out path exists.**

`withdraw_wnear_to_router` sends wNEAR *to* the router (for attached NEAR on outgoing calls); it does not withdraw NEAR *from* the router back to the user: [7](#0-6) 

There is no `close_router`, `withdraw_storage`, or equivalent function exposed anywhere in the EVM interface. Once the router is created, the 2 NEAR is inaccessible through Aurora.

Additionally, old routers that predate the upgradable version are explicitly marked as permanently non-upgradable, compounding the inability to add new functionality retroactively: [8](#0-7) 

---

### Impact Explanation

**High — Permanent freezing of funds.**

Every EVM user who first uses the XCC precompile loses 2 NEAR worth of wNEAR with no EVM-accessible recovery path. The NEAR is held as storage-staking collateral in the router sub-account (`{address_hex}.{engine_account_id}`). The router can only be interacted with through the engine (which only exposes `execute`/`schedule`), and the user's EVM address has no corresponding NEAR account that could call the router directly. The 2 NEAR is permanently frozen from the EVM user's perspective.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**High.** The charge is automatic and unavoidable on every user's first XCC call. There is no preview, confirmation, or opt-out step. Any EVM user who wishes to use cross-contract calls will trigger this deduction.

---

### Recommendation

1. Add a `withdraw_storage` (or equivalent) method to the XCC router and expose it through the precompile, allowing users to recover their storage-staking NEAR (converted back to wNEAR) when they no longer need the router.
2. Alternatively, document the 2 NEAR permanent cost prominently so users are aware before their first XCC call.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Alice holds wNEAR on Aurora and calls the XCC precompile for the first time with `attached_near = 0`.
2. The precompile computes `required_near = 0 + STORAGE_AMOUNT = 2 NEAR`.
3. The precompile calls `wNEAR.transferFrom(Alice, engine_implicit_address, 2 NEAR)` — Alice's wNEAR balance drops by 2 NEAR.
4. The engine deploys the router at `{alice_hex}.aurora` and transfers 2 NEAR to it as storage staking.
5. Alice later wants her 2 NEAR back. She inspects the XCC precompile: only `execute` and `schedule` are constructible.
6. She inspects `engine/src/xcc.rs`: `withdraw_wnear_to_router` sends NEAR *to* the router, not back to her.
7. She has no NEAR account corresponding to her EVM address that could call the router directly.
8. The 2 NEAR is permanently inaccessible through the Aurora EVM interface.

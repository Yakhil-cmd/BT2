### Title
Unhandled Transfer Failure in wNEAR Unwrap Path Causes Permanent Fund Freeze — (`engine-precompiles/src/native.rs` + `engine/src/contract_methods/connector.rs`)

### Summary

When a user calls `withdrawToNear` with the `:unwrap` suffix on the wNEAR ERC-20 mirror, the engine constructs a two-step promise chain: (1) `near_withdraw` on the wNEAR NEP-141 contract, then (2) a `PromiseBatchAction::Transfer` to the user-supplied `target_account_id`. If step (1) succeeds but step (2) fails (e.g., `target_account_id` is a syntactically valid but non-existent named NEAR account), the NEAR protocol refunds the NEAR back to the Aurora contract's balance. There is no further callback or recovery mechanism. The user's ERC-20 and wNEAR tokens are already burned, and the NEAR is permanently unattributed inside Aurora's balance.

### Finding Description

**Step 1 — Precompile entry (`exit_erc20_token_to_near`):**

In `exit_erc20_token_to_near`, when `Message::UnwrapWnear` is matched and the caller is the wNEAR ERC-20 address, the engine builds:
- A `near_withdraw` call on the wNEAR NEP-141 contract (base promise)
- A `TransferNearArgs { target_account_id, amount }` stored in `ExitToNearPrecompileCallbackArgs` [1](#0-0) 

**Step 2 — Promise construction:**

The precompile wraps these into a `PromiseWithCallbackArgs`:
- Base: `near_withdraw` on wNEAR
- Callback: `exit_to_near_precompile_callback` on Aurora engine [2](#0-1) 

**Step 3 — Callback (`exit_to_near_precompile_callback`):**

If `near_withdraw` succeeds, the callback fires a `PromiseBatchAction::Transfer` to `target_account_id`. This is a **fire-and-forget** promise — there is no nested callback to handle its failure: [3](#0-2) 

The `else if` branch (refund path) is only reachable when `near_withdraw` itself fails, not when the subsequent Transfer fails: [4](#0-3) 

**Step 4 — No validation of `target_account_id` existence:**

`receiver_account_id` is only validated for syntactic correctness (valid `AccountId` format), not for on-chain existence: [5](#0-4) 

**Step 5 — `error_refund` feature does not cover this path:**

The `error_refund` feature populates `RefundCallArgs` only to handle `near_withdraw` failure. Even when enabled, it does not add a callback on the Transfer promise: [6](#0-5) [7](#0-6) 

### Impact Explanation

When the Transfer to a non-existent named NEAR account fails, NEAR protocol refunds the NEAR to the Aurora contract's balance (the predecessor of the receipt). At this point:
- The user's wNEAR ERC-20 tokens are burned (irreversible)
- The wNEAR NEP-141 tokens are burned (irreversible)
- The NEAR is in Aurora's general balance with no attribution to the user and no recovery path

This constitutes **permanent freezing of user funds** (or at minimum High-severity temporary freezing requiring admin intervention to recover).

### Likelihood Explanation

Any EVM user holding wNEAR ERC-20 tokens can trigger this by calling `withdrawToNear` with `<nonexistent_account>:unwrap`. This can happen accidentally (typo in account name) or deliberately. No special privileges are required. The `target_account_id` is fully attacker-controlled and only syntactically validated.

### Recommendation

Add a callback on the `PromiseBatchAction::Transfer` promise in `exit_to_near_precompile_callback`. If the Transfer fails, the callback should re-mint the equivalent ERC-20 tokens to the original sender (similar to the existing `refund_on_error` mechanism). Alternatively, validate that `target_account_id` exists before proceeding (though this requires an additional async lookup). At minimum, the Transfer promise should have a failure handler that re-credits the user's EVM balance.

### Proof of Concept

1. Deploy a local Aurora sandbox with wNEAR ERC-20 mirror configured.
2. Mint wNEAR ERC-20 tokens to an EVM address `A`.
3. From `A`, call `IExit.withdrawToNear(bytes("nonexistent_account_xyz.near:unwrap"), amount)` on the wNEAR ERC-20 contract.
4. Observe: ERC-20 balance of `A` decremented, wNEAR NEP-141 burned, `near_withdraw` succeeds.
5. Observe: `exit_to_near_precompile_callback` fires the Transfer to `nonexistent_account_xyz.near`.
6. Observe: Transfer receipt fails (account does not exist); NEAR refunded to Aurora contract balance.
7. Assert: `A`'s ERC-20 balance is zero, `nonexistent_account_xyz.near` received no NEAR, Aurora's NEAR balance increased by `amount` with no user-accessible recovery path. [8](#0-7)

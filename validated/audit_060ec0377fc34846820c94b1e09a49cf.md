### Title
NEP-141 Transfer-Fee Tokens Cause ERC-20 Mirror Over-Minting and Protocol Insolvency - (File: `engine/src/engine.rs`)

---

### Summary

`receive_erc20_tokens` and `receive_base_tokens` in `engine/src/engine.rs` unconditionally mint the caller-supplied `args.amount` of ERC-20 / base tokens on Aurora without verifying the actual NEP-141 amount received after any on-token transfer fee. A NEP-141 token with a transfer fee can be bridged to Aurora, causing Aurora to mint more ERC-20 mirror tokens than it holds NEP-141 backing for, making the bridge insolvent for that token.

---

### Finding Description

Aurora's bridge entry point for NEP-141 tokens is `ft_on_transfer` in `engine/src/contract_methods/connector.rs`. It is called by the NEP-141 contract after `ft_transfer_call` completes. The function reads the JSON-encoded `FtOnTransferArgs` (which contains `amount` as reported by the NEP-141 contract) and dispatches to either `receive_base_tokens` or `receive_erc20_tokens` in `engine/src/engine.rs`.

**`receive_base_tokens`** (ETH bridge path): [1](#0-0) 

**`receive_erc20_tokens`** (NEP-141 → ERC-20 bridge path): [2](#0-1) 

In both cases the code uses `args.amount` — the amount the *sender* specified in `ft_transfer_call` — as the quantity to mint. It never compares the contract's NEP-141 balance before and after the transfer to determine the *actual* amount received.

Under the NEP-141 standard, `ft_on_transfer` is called with the `amount` field set to the value the sender passed to `ft_transfer_call`. If the NEP-141 token implementation deducts a transfer fee before crediting the receiver, the actual tokens held by Aurora will be `amount - fee`, but Aurora mints the full `amount` of ERC-20 tokens.

The dispatcher in `ft_on_transfer` that routes to these two functions: [3](#0-2) 

The exit path (`withdrawToNear` → `ExitToNear` precompile → `ft_transfer` on the NEP-141) transfers the full burned ERC-20 amount back to the NEAR recipient: [4](#0-3) 

Because the exit path requests the full ERC-20 amount from the NEP-141 contract, and Aurora holds fewer NEP-141 tokens than the total ERC-20 supply, the NEP-141 `ft_transfer` calls will eventually fail for the last users to exit, permanently freezing their ERC-20 tokens.

---

### Impact Explanation

**Critical — Insolvency / Permanent fund freeze.**

For every `ft_transfer_call` of a fee-bearing NEP-141 token into Aurora, the ERC-20 mirror supply grows by `amount` while the NEP-141 reserve grows by only `amount - fee`. The deficit accumulates with each deposit. When users attempt to exit via `withdrawToNear`, the NEP-141 `ft_transfer` will revert for the last depositors because Aurora's NEP-141 balance is insufficient. Their ERC-20 tokens are burned but the NEP-141 transfer fails; with the `error_refund` feature the ERC-20 tokens are re-minted, but the underlying NEP-141 deficit remains, making full redemption impossible for all holders collectively.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Medium.** Any NEP-141 token can be permissionlessly bridged to Aurora via `deploy_erc20_token`. A token that introduces a transfer fee after deployment (or is deployed with one from the start) immediately creates the accounting gap. No privileged access is required; a normal token holder calling `ft_transfer_call` is the only actor needed. The impact grows linearly with deposit volume.

---

### Recommendation

In `receive_erc20_tokens` and `receive_base_tokens`, record the Aurora-side NEP-141 balance (or ETH connector balance) before the transfer and compute the actual credited amount by comparing balances after the callback, then mint only that delta. Alternatively, reject `ft_on_transfer` calls from NEP-141 tokens that are known to carry transfer fees, enforced via an allowlist checked at `deploy_erc20_token` time.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Deploy a NEP-141 token `fee_token.near` that deducts a 1% fee on every `ft_transfer` / `ft_transfer_call`.
2. Call `deploy_erc20_token` on Aurora to create the ERC-20 mirror `FEE_ERC20`.
3. Call `fee_token.near::ft_transfer_call(receiver_id: "aurora", amount: "1000", msg: "<evm_address>")`.
   - `fee_token.near` credits Aurora with `990` tokens (1% fee deducted).
   - `fee_token.near` calls `aurora::ft_on_transfer(sender_id, amount: "1000", msg: "<evm_address>")`.
4. Aurora's `receive_erc20_tokens` reads `args.amount = 1000` and mints `1000` `FEE_ERC20` to `<evm_address>`. [5](#0-4) 
5. Aurora now holds `990` `fee_token.near` but has `1000` `FEE_ERC20` outstanding — a deficit of `10`.
6. Repeat N times; deficit grows to `10 * N`.
7. The last depositor calls `FEE_ERC20::withdrawToNear(recipient, amount)`. Aurora burns the ERC-20 and calls `fee_token.near::ft_transfer(recipient, amount)`. The call reverts because Aurora's `fee_token.near` balance is exhausted. The depositor's funds are permanently frozen. [4](#0-3)

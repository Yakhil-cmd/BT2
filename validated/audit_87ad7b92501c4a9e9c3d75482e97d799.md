Audit Report

## Title
Neurons' Fund Neuron Can Avoid SNS Swap Maturity Draw by Leaving and Rejoining the Community Fund - (File: rs/nns/governance/src/neuron/types.rs)

## Summary
A Neurons' Fund neuron controller can observe a pending `CreateServiceNervousSystem` proposal during its public voting period, call `LeaveCommunityFund` before execution, avoid having their maturity drawn for the SNS swap, and immediately rejoin the fund afterward. No timelock or cooldown exists on either operation. This reduces total Neurons' Fund participation in SNS swaps and shifts a disproportionate maturity burden onto remaining participants.

## Finding Description
`draw_maturity_from_neurons_fund` in `rs/nns/governance/src/governance.rs` (L7381) calls `self.neuron_store.list_active_neurons_fund_neurons()` at proposal **execution** time, not at submission time. The snapshot therefore reflects only neurons currently in the fund when the proposal executes.

`leave_community_fund` in `rs/nns/governance/src/neuron/types.rs` (L613â€“620) simply sets `joined_community_fund_timestamp_seconds = None` with no timelock, cooldown, or check for pending proposals. `join_community_fund` (L601â€“609) similarly sets the timestamp unconditionally. There is no guard preventing immediate re-entry after leaving.

The proportional maturity calculation in `rs/nns/governance/src/neurons_fund.rs` (L1094â€“1107) computes each neuron's share as `neuron_maturity / total_fund_maturity * intended_participation`. When a large neuron exits before execution, `total_fund_maturity` decreases, raising every remaining neuron's proportional share and reducing the total NF contribution to the swap.

Exploit path:
1. A `CreateServiceNervousSystem` proposal with `neurons_fund_participation = true` is submitted.
2. During the multi-day public voting period, the attacker calls `manage_neuron` â†’ `Configure` â†’ `LeaveCommunityFund`.
3. The proposal is adopted and executed; `list_active_neurons_fund_neurons()` does not include the attacker's neuron; no maturity is drawn from it.
4. The attacker immediately calls `JoinCommunityFund` to rejoin.
5. Remaining NF neurons each bear a larger proportional share; the SNS swap receives less NF participation than intended.

No existing check prevents this: `leave_community_fund` and `join_community_fund` are unconditional state mutations with no awareness of pending proposals.

## Impact Explanation
This constitutes a significant NNS/SNS governance accounting impact with concrete protocol harm. The matched-funding mechanism is systematically undermined: SNS projects receive less Neurons' Fund participation than the protocol intends, and remaining NF participants bear unfair maturity costs. The attacker retains full NF membership benefits (voting rewards) between swaps while contributing nothing to the swaps themselves. This qualifies as a **High** impact: significant NNS/SNS security impact with concrete user and protocol harm.

## Likelihood Explanation
The attack requires no privileged access. NNS proposals are fully public with a voting period of multiple days. The attacker needs only to monitor the NNS proposal queue and submit two standard unprivileged `manage_neuron` ingress calls. This is trivially automatable and repeatable across every SNS swap. Any Neurons' Fund neuron controller can execute this.

## Recommendation
Take the Neurons' Fund snapshot at **proposal submission time** rather than execution time, and lock participating neurons' membership for the duration of the swap. Alternatively, enforce a timelock (e.g., 7 days) on `LeaveCommunityFund` so that a neuron cannot exit and immediately avoid a pending draw. The snapshot should be stored in `ProposalData` at proposal creation and used directly by `draw_maturity_from_neurons_fund` instead of calling `list_active_neurons_fund_neurons()` at execution time.

## Proof of Concept
1. Deploy a local replica or PocketIC instance with NNS governance.
2. Create a Neurons' Fund neuron with significant maturity.
3. Submit a `CreateServiceNervousSystem` proposal with `neurons_fund_participation = true`.
4. Before the proposal executes, call `manage_neuron` with `Operation::LeaveCommunityFund({})` from the neuron's controller.
5. Allow the proposal to execute; verify via `get_proposal_info` that `neurons_fund_data.initial_neurons_fund_participation` does not include the neuron.
6. Call `manage_neuron` with `Operation::JoinCommunityFund({})` immediately after; verify the neuron is back in the fund.
7. Confirm the neuron's maturity was not reduced and that remaining NF neurons each contributed a larger share than they would have with the attacker present.

### Title
Integer Division Truncation in XCC Gas Conversion Undercharges EVM Gas for NEAR Gas Consumed - (File: engine-precompiles/src/xcc.rs)

### Summary
The Cross-Contract Call (XCC) precompile converts NEAR gas attached to a promise into an EVM gas cost using integer division. Because integer division truncates toward zero, the EVM gas charged to the caller is always rounded **down**, allowing callers to consume up to `CROSS_CONTRACT_CALL_NEAR_GAS - 1` NEAR gas units without paying the corresponding EVM gas. This is the direct analog of the `BaseSlippageHooks.sol` bug: a threshold/cost check uses integer division that rounds down, allowing the user to pay less than intended.

### Finding Description

In `engine-precompiles/src/xcc.rs` at line 174, the EVM gas cost for the NEAR gas attached to an XCC promise is computed as:

```rust
cost += EthGas::new(promise.attached_gas.as_u64() / costs::CROSS_CONTRACT_CALL_NEAR_GAS);
``` [1](#0-0) 

`promise.attached_gas` is the NEAR gas the caller requests to attach to the outgoing cross-contract call. `CROSS_CONTRACT_CALL_NEAR_GAS` is the conversion factor (NEAR gas units per EVM gas unit). Because Rust integer division truncates, the EVM gas charged is:

```
floor(promise.attached_gas / CROSS_CONTRACT_CALL_NEAR_GAS)
```

rather than the exact (or ceiling) value. The maximum underpayment per call is `CROSS_CONTRACT_CALL_NEAR_GAS - 1` EVM gas units. A caller who sets `promise.attached_gas = N * CROSS_CONTRACT_CALL_NEAR_GAS - 1` for any positive integer `N` pays `N - 1` EVM gas units instead of `N`, consuming one full unit of NEAR gas "for free."

The analogous fix (multiply before dividing, or use ceiling division) would be:

```rust
// Avoid truncation: charge ceiling instead of floor
cost += EthGas::new(
    promise.attached_gas.as_u64().div_ceil(costs::CROSS_CONTRACT_CALL_NEAR_GAS)
);
```

The XCC precompile is reached via `submit_with_args` / `submit`, which is the standard EVM transaction entry point. [2](#0-1) 

The `promise.attached_gas` value is fully attacker-controlled: it comes from the ABI-encoded `CrossContractCallArgs` in the EVM calldata. [3](#0-2) 

### Impact Explanation

The Aurora Engine's NEAR balance is used to pay for NEAR gas consumed by XCC calls. EVM gas fees collected from callers are supposed to reimburse this cost. When the EVM gas charged is rounded down, the Aurora Engine absorbs the difference from its own NEAR balance. Repeated calls with carefully chosen `attached_gas` values (just below a multiple of `CROSS_CONTRACT_CALL_NEAR_GAS`) drain the Aurora Engine's NEAR balance faster than EVM gas fees replenish it. This constitutes a gas/fee-accounting bug leading to protocol-level fund loss (theft of unclaimed yield / path toward insolvency).

**Impact: High** — Theft of unclaimed yield (Aurora Engine gas fee revenue is systematically undercharged).

### Likelihood Explanation

Any unprivileged EVM user or contract can call the XCC precompile by sending a transaction to the Aurora Engine with calldata targeting the XCC precompile address. The truncation is triggered whenever `promise.attached_gas % CROSS_CONTRACT_CALL_NEAR_GAS != 0`, which is the common case for arbitrary NEAR gas values. No special privileges are required.

### Recommendation

Replace the truncating integer division with ceiling division to ensure the caller always pays at least as much EVM gas as the NEAR gas they consume:

```rust
// Before (truncates, undercharges):
cost += EthGas::new(promise.attached_gas.as_u64() / costs::CROSS_CONTRACT_CALL_NEAR_GAS);

// After (ceiling division, no undercharge):
cost += EthGas::new(
    promise.attached_gas.as_u64().div_ceil(costs::CROSS_CONTRACT_CALL_NEAR_GAS)
);
```

Alternatively, restructure the comparison to avoid division entirely, analogous to the `BaseSlippageHooks.sol` fix.

### Proof of Concept

1. Attacker deploys an EVM contract on Aurora that calls the XCC precompile.
2. The contract constructs a `CrossContractCallArgs::Eager` payload with `attached_gas = CROSS_CONTRACT_CALL_NEAR_GAS - 1` (just below one conversion unit).
3. After line 174, `promise.attached_gas.as_u64() / CROSS_CONTRACT_CALL_NEAR_GAS = 0`, so **zero** EVM gas is added to `cost` for the NEAR gas component.
4. The Aurora Engine executes the XCC call, consuming `CROSS_CONTRACT_CALL_NEAR_GAS - 1` NEAR gas units from its balance, but the caller paid 0 EVM gas for it.
5. Repeating this across many transactions drains the Aurora Engine's NEAR balance without corresponding EVM gas fee collection. [4](#0-3)

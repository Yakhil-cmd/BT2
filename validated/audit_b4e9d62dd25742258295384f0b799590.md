### Title
Staker Can Instantly Raise Commission to Maximum via `set_commission_commitment` + `set_commission` With No Delegator Exit Window — (File: `src/staking/staking.cairo`)

---

### Summary

A staker can atomically set a commission commitment and immediately invoke `set_commission` to raise their commission to any value up to `COMMISSION_DENOMINATOR` (100%) in the same block. Because delegators are bound by the `exit_wait_window` (up to 21 days), they cannot exit before the higher commission rate begins consuming their yield. The codebase itself acknowledges this gap in a developer note.

---

### Finding Description

The protocol normally prevents commission increases: `set_commission` requires `commission < old_commission` when no active commitment exists. [1](#0-0) 

However, `set_commission_commitment` creates an exception: once a commitment is active, `set_commission` may raise commission up to `max_commission`. [2](#0-1) 

The spec requires only that `expiration_epoch > current_epoch` (i.e., `current_epoch + 1` is the minimum), and the commitment becomes active immediately upon being written. [3](#0-2) 

There is **no minimum delay** between calling `set_commission_commitment` and calling `set_commission` to raise the commission. A staker can execute both in the same block:

1. `set_commission_commitment(max_commission: 10000, expiration_epoch: current_epoch + 1)`
2. `set_commission(commission: 10000)` — raises commission to 100% immediately

The developer note in the implementation explicitly acknowledges this gap: [4](#0-3) 

> "Current commission increase safeguards still allow for sudden commission changes."

The commission is written directly to storage with no epoch-delay buffer: [5](#0-4) 

---

### Impact Explanation

**High — Theft of unclaimed yield.**

Delegators who are currently staked cannot exit immediately; they are subject to the `exit_wait_window`. [6](#0-5) 

During the entire exit window, all rewards accruing to the pool are split at the new (100%) commission rate, meaning delegators receive 0% of yield they would otherwise have earned. This constitutes theft of unclaimed yield for the duration of the forced lock-in period.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Medium-High.** Any staker who has attracted delegators has a direct financial incentive to execute this two-transaction sequence. No special access, leaked key, or external dependency is required — only the staker's own address. The attack is permissionless from the staker's perspective and requires no coordination.

---

### Recommendation

Enforce a minimum delay between the moment a commitment is set and the moment it may be used to *increase* commission. For example:

- Require `expiration_epoch >= current_epoch + MIN_COMMITMENT_NOTICE_EPOCHS` where `MIN_COMMITMENT_NOTICE_EPOCHS` is large enough for delegators to observe the pending increase and submit an exit intent.
- Alternatively, make commission increases take effect only at the start of the epoch *after* the one in which `set_commission` is called, giving delegators at least one full epoch of notice.

---

### Proof of Concept

```
Epoch N:
  tx1: staker calls set_commission_commitment(max_commission=10000, expiration_epoch=N+1)
       → commitment is immediately active (expiration_epoch > current_epoch ✓)

  tx2 (same block): staker calls set_commission(commission=10000)
       → commitment exists and commission(10000) <= max_commission(10000) ✓
       → commission written to storage immediately

Epoch N → N + exit_wait_window:
  Delegators earn 0% of pool rewards (100% goes to staker).
  Delegators cannot exit until exit_wait_window elapses.
  All yield accrued during this window is stolen by the staker.
```

The two-step sequence is confirmed valid by the spec pre-conditions: [7](#0-6) [8](#0-7)

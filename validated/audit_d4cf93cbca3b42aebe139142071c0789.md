### Title
EVM Global Pause Overly Restricts COA Asset Withdrawal, Trapping User Funds - (File: `fvm/evm/stdlib/contract.cdc`)

---

### Summary

The `EVM` contract's global `isPaused()` guard is applied uniformly to every state-mutating operation, including the fund-exit paths `CadenceOwnedAccount.withdraw()`, `withdrawNFT()`, and `withdrawTokens()`. When the Governance Committee activates the pause, every COA owner is simultaneously locked out of their own FLOW tokens and bridged assets with no escape route, mirroring the external report's "pause overly restricts liquidity removal" class.

---

### Finding Description

`EVM.isPaused()` reads a boolean from `/storage/evmOperationsPaused` on the EVM contract account. [1](#0-0) 

Every state-mutating entry point in the contract carries an identical `pre { !EVM.isPaused() }` guard:

| Function | Line |
|---|---|
| `EVMAddress.deposit()` | 203–205 |
| `CadenceOwnedAccount.withdraw()` | 588–590 |
| `CadenceOwnedAccount.deploy()` | 623–625 |
| `CadenceOwnedAccount.call()` | 643–645 |
| `CadenceOwnedAccount.callWithSigAndArgs()` | 669–671 |
| `CadenceOwnedAccount.depositNFT()` | 739–741 |
| `CadenceOwnedAccount.withdrawNFT()` | 761–763 |
| `CadenceOwnedAccount.depositTokens()` | 778–780 |
| `CadenceOwnedAccount.withdrawTokens()` | 793–795 |
| `EVM.createCadenceOwnedAccount()` | 808–810 |
| `EVM.run()` / `EVM.batchRun()` | 829–831 / 919–921 | [2](#0-1) [3](#0-2) [4](#0-3) 

The design intent documented in the contract is that the pause creates a "read-only mode": [5](#0-4) 

However, **withdrawal** is not a write into the EVM state from the perspective of a user protecting their assets — it is an exit. Blocking it is architecturally equivalent to the external report's `removeLiquidity()` being disabled during a pause. The user's FLOW tokens remain locked inside the EVM sub-state with no Cadence-side escape path.

`CadenceOwnedAccount.deposit()` (the COA wrapper) has **no** `isPaused()` guard of its own: [6](#0-5) 

It delegates to `EVMAddress.deposit()`, which does carry the guard (line 203–205). The asymmetry is therefore complete: neither deposits nor withdrawals are possible during a pause, but the design comment implies only new state mutations should be blocked.

---

### Impact Explanation

Any user who holds FLOW tokens, ERC-20 tokens, or NFTs inside a `CadenceOwnedAccount` loses access to those assets for the entire duration of the pause. Because COAs are the **only** Cadence-controlled EVM wallet type, there is no alternative withdrawal path. Assets cannot be moved to a different address, bridged back, or otherwise recovered until the Governance Committee explicitly unpauses. If the pause is triggered during a period of market stress or protocol incident, users bear the full price-movement risk of their locked EVM positions with no ability to exit.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The Governance Committee can pause the EVM via a multi-sig Cadence transaction at any time for maintenance or emergency response. The contract explicitly anticipates this use: [7](#0-6) 

The bootstrap code also pauses the bridge during setup, confirming the pause path is exercised in practice: [8](#0-7) 

A pause event is therefore a realistic, planned operational action, not a theoretical edge case.

---

### Recommendation

Introduce a finer-grained pause flag — analogous to the report's `slashingAvailableStatus` — that distinguishes between:

- **Inbound operations** (new deposits, `run`, `batchRun`, `deploy`, `call`, `createCadenceOwnedAccount`) — blocked during pause.
- **Exit operations** (`withdraw`, `withdrawNFT`, `withdrawTokens`) — **exempt** from the pause guard so users can always recover their assets.

Concretely, remove the `!EVM.isPaused()` pre-condition from `CadenceOwnedAccount.withdraw()`, `withdrawNFT()`, and `withdrawTokens()`, and introduce a separate `isWithdrawalPaused` flag if a stricter emergency mode is ever needed.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Governance Committee submits a multi-sig transaction saving `true` to `/storage/evmOperationsPaused` on the EVM contract account (as exercised in `TestEVMPauseFunctionality`). [9](#0-8) 

2. Any user who owns a COA with a non-zero FLOW balance attempts:
   ```cadence
   let vault <- coa.withdraw(balance: bal)
   ```

3. The `pre` block in `CadenceOwnedAccount.withdraw()` evaluates `EVM.isPaused()` → `true` and panics with `"EVM operations are temporarily paused"`. [10](#0-9) 

4. The test confirms this revert: [11](#0-10) 

5. The user's FLOW tokens remain trapped in the EVM sub-state for the entire pause duration with no alternative exit path.
